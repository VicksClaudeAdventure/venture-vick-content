This is how I would rank the social media strategies
you see online, copying other creators.
I would put this as B tier because
copying word for word is always bad,
but studying top performers and repurposing their formats
is a really good first step to understanding good content.
Hashtags I would put into C tier
because they're not as important for growth
as they were once before.
However, they still do help categorize
your content for SEO purposes.
Another strategy that you hear is that every post
is designed to go viral.
I would put this as D tier because
it only serves one of the four missions.
It will attract attention,
but your other posts still need to nurture,
position and convert as well.
Posting daily, I am going to put at A tier
because quantity gives you more chances for success.
But it's not S tier because you can't simply post
the same thing over and over again
and expecting a different result.
You have to be thoughtful by changing something
for each iteration.
Copping trends I would put into C tier,
they actually can be really helpful
for gaining new attention.
If you repurpose the format for a brand-building experience
like educating your audience,
but if your whole page is just trends
then you risk having no meaningful connection.
Now story telling definitely S tier.
Traditional content tells you to educate others
on what to do.
But when you start to tell stories,
you explain why that topic is actually meaningful to you.
This shift is how you get people to not only love
your information, but love you just as much.
