What video do you think performed better?
Video A or video B?
The answer is video B, but why?
They both follow the Talking Back and Fourth format,
but only one includes multitasking as well.
While Talking Back and Fourth is one of the most
effective engaging formats you can use for a video,
I've noticed all the best include one of these three things.
Number one, multitasking, doing something unrelated
in the video while saying the script.
Number two, visual.
This is where you use props, objects, or movements
in the video that are related to your script.
Number three is editing notes.
This is visuals edited onto the screen,
showing what's being talked about.
Essentially, this is what I would call format stacking,
and almost all the best Talking Back and Fourth videos
you use it, follow for more social media marketing,
education.
