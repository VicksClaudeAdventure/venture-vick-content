## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
Can you build a budget when you don't know how much money is coming in next month?

📷 *Calendar with three different-sized dollar bars per week, inconsistent — pattern break*

[Answer — the thesis]
Yes. But you can't use a normal budget. You need a base-layer budget.

📷 *Text on screen: "Base-layer budget"*

[Problem setup]
Every budget video tells you to add up your monthly income and split it into categories. That math only works if the number at the top is the same every month. Mine isn't.

📷 *Calendar with three months showing wildly different incomes*

[Step 1]
Step one — find your floor. The lowest income month you've had in the last year. That's your baseline. Pretend that's all you make. Build your must-pay list against that number — rent, food, insurance, minimum payments. Nothing else.

📷 *Screen: "Floor: [$ amount]" with must-pay list under it*

[Step 2]
Step two — everything above the floor gets a job before it hits your account. Not "extra." Not "maybe save." A pre-decided job. Mine is split three ways: half to the Recovery number, a quarter to taxes, a quarter to runway.

📷 *Pie chart: 50% / 25% / 25% labeled*

[Step 3 / CTA]
Step three is the part nobody talks about. Save this — I'm doing a full breakdown with my real numbers next week.

📷 *Jay at laptop, calm*
