## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
I spent more on one thing last month than I paid in rent and I didn't notice until I wrote it all down.

📷 *Phone/laptop screen: spending spreadsheet, one row glowing red*

[Agitate]
I've been tracking every dollar since January. Because I'm trying to make back thirty-four thousand dollars I wasted the last three years.

📷 *Recovery counter: $34,776 → current*

[Context]
Most months the tracker feels routine. But last month one number was bigger than everything else and I had to look twice.

📷 *Spreadsheet close-up: categories with amounts*

[Reveal]
Rent for my spot is around eight hundred dollars. The category that beat it? Food. Specifically food I didn't cook. Delivery, takeout, "I'll just grab something real quick" — eleven hundred dollars.

📷 *Screen: "Food out: $1,102" in red, "Rent: $820" below*

[How-to / lesson]
The spending that kills you is never the big stuff. It's the nineteen-dollar Uber Eats orders you stopped counting.

📷 *Rapid cuts: ~6 Uber Eats receipts flashing*

[CTA]
I'm dropping the rule I used to cut it in half next week. Save this so you have it when I do.

📷 *Jay at laptop, mug in hand*
