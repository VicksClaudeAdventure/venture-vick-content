## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
Three guys, one mortgage, twenty-four years old. Two summers ago we signed and I've been waiting to tell you what happened.

📷 *Exterior: actual house in Philly, text overlay "Summer 2024"*

[Doubt]
Our parents said we'd lose the friendship. Our coworkers said we were too young. One person literally told us three people on a mortgage is legally insane.

📷 *Jay walking toward house, slow-motion approach*

[Struggle — the reality]
And they weren't wrong about all of it. Three people means three opinions on every wall color, every repair, and every dollar.

📷 *Interior shots: renovation chaos, paint cans, tools*

[Turning point — the numbers]
Three forty-five thousand. Ten percent down, split three ways — about eleven-five each. Monthly mortgage twenty-six forty-nine. My share? Under a thousand bucks a month.

📷 *Numbers animating in: $345,000 → 10% → $11,500 each → $883/mo*

[Transformation]
Almost two years later the house is worth about forty thousand more than we paid. We're still friends. We're still arguing about the thermostat.

📷 *Interior: renovated house looking great*

[CTA — binary outcome]
Next week I'm showing you how we actually split the money. The split isn't equal — and that's on purpose. Follow to see if you think it's fair.

📷 *Text: "37.5% / 37.5% / 25% — is this fair?"*
