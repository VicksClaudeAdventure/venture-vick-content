## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
I told the internet three of us bought a house together and everyone wanted to know how we actually split the money.

📷 *Phone with comments scrolling: "how does the split work?" "do you all pay equally?"*

[Setup]
So here's the full breakdown. The ownership split is 37.5, 37.5, and 25. It's not equal. Here's why.

📷 *Pie chart: 37.5 / 37.5 / 25 — color-coded to three people*

[Step 1 — the mortgage]
One shared bank account. First of every month, each person transfers their share. Twenty-six forty-nine split three ways means my third is eight eighty-three.

📷 *Transfer notification: "$883.00 — Monthly share"*

[Step 2 — the repairs]
When something breaks — and it will — we discuss it in the group chat, agree on the fix, and split the cost. Last month: gutter repair. Four hundred bucks. One thirty-three each.

📷 *Group chat screenshot: "gutter = $400, $133 each?"*

[Step 3 — the rent]
When rent comes in it sits in the shared account and covers the next month's expenses. Whatever's left gets divided by ownership percentage.

📷 *Spreadsheet: income row → expense row → remainder row → percentage split*

[Lesson]
The key is everything's automated and documented. Nobody has to ask anyone for money. Nobody feels weird. It's a system, not a handshake.

📷 *Jay at laptop, calm*

[CTA]
Next — how we blew our renovation budget by twenty-five thousand dollars. Follow if you want to see where it went.
