## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
My mom never heard the word startup and she's the reason mine is still alive.

📷 *Old family photo or Jay as a kid with his mom — pattern break via intimacy*

[Setback]
I've spent the last four years consuming every startup podcast, every business book, every thread on how to build a company. I thought I was preparing myself.

📷 *Quick montage: podcast app, book stack, Twitter scroll*

[Pain points]
But when I actually started building — when it was me alone at a table with a broken app and no users — none of it helped. Not one framework. Not one playbook. Not one five-step strategy.

📷 *Jay at kitchen table, alone, laptop*

[Resolution]
What helped was something my mom told me when I was a kid. She said — when something is hard, you don't wait until it gets easy. You just keep showing up until you're the kind of person who doesn't quit.

📷 *Jay talking direct to camera, softer register*

[Lesson]
She didn't know what a startup was. She didn't know what product-market fit meant. But she taught me the only thing that actually matters when you're building something from nothing: you don't stop.

📷 *Jay looking off camera, reflective*

[CTA]
Follow to watch me try not to quit this thing in real time.

📷 *Jay slight nod*
