## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
Four years of business podcasts taught me less than two weeks of actually building something that doesn't work.

📷 *Split screen: left = podcast app + books scrolling / right = laptop with code errors — visual contrast*

[Problem]
I read the books. I listened to everything on my commute. I followed every founder who posts advice. And I genuinely believed I was getting ready.

📷 *Montage: earbuds in, scrolling Twitter, highlighting a book*

[Problem — the realization]
I was not getting ready. I was just getting comfortable being someone who talks about building things without building anything.

📷 *Jay at camera, direct, honest*

[Pursuit]
Two weeks ago I started. Actually started. Built an app. Put it in front of strangers. Watched them break it. Fixed it. Shipped it.

📷 *Rapid cuts: laptop typing, user testing, code fixing, shipping*

[Payoff — the three lessons]
In two weeks I learned three things no podcast ever told me. Your first users don't care about your features — they care if it works. Your pricing is wrong the first time, guaranteed. And the hardest part isn't building — it's telling people the thing exists.

📷 *3 bullets appearing on screen one at a time*

[CTA]
This is week one of actually doing it. Follow to see how many weeks I last before I realize the podcasts were right about something.

📷 *Jay slight smirk*
