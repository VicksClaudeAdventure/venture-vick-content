## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
An AI built my entire app. I refused to put my name on it until I broke five things.

📷 *Split screen: left side "AI's version" screenshot / right side "Jay's version" — pattern break*

[Intro — the setup]
By Day nine the whole thing was technically working. Landlord stuff, flagging, results page, everything. And I hated it.

📷 *Screen recording: scrolling through AI's original UI*

[Inflection]
Not because it was broken. Because it didn't feel like me. It felt like a template.

📷 *Close-up: Jay at laptop, hand on chin, frustrated*

[Rising action — the 5]
So I made a rule. I couldn't show it to anyone until I'd broken five things about it and rebuilt them. By hand. No AI.

📷 *Whiteboard or notes: "5 things to break" written, numbered 1-5*

[Climax — the breaks]
I rewrote the upload flow. I redid the language — the AI was using words a law student would use, not someone who's actually renting. I killed two buttons. I killed a whole feature. And I rebuilt the results page from scratch.

📷 *Rapid cuts: 5 before/after screens, one for each change*

[Resolution]
The weird thing is the app's almost identical. But now I feel like I can actually send it to someone.

📷 *Jay at laptop, calmer*

[CTA]
Tomorrow I'm showing you the part where I finished it and then hid from the internet for three days. Follow to see what happened.

📷 *Jay closing laptop, slight nervous smile*
