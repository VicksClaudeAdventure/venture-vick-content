## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
Have you ever finished building something and immediately been too scared to show anyone?

📷 *Close-up on Jay's face, thoughtful, natural light, face in frame*

[Context]
Because I've been asking myself this all week.

📷 *Jay at laptop, app open on screen but not shared*

[Problem]
I finished the app. It works. The 84% bug is fixed. The five things I hated are fixed. I could post about it right now.

📷 *Phone screen: draft post written, unposted, cursor blinking*

[Pursuit — the avoidance]
But for three days I've been finding reasons not to. One more bug fix. One more button. One more test run.

📷 *Rapid cuts: settings changes, small UI tweaks, file saves*

[Re-hook — the confession]
And I know what I'm doing. I'm not perfecting it. I'm hiding.

📷 *Jay direct to camera, quiet honesty*

[Payoff — the open question]
Because once I show it, it stops being the thing in my head and starts being the thing people have opinions about. And I don't know how to tell if I'm afraid of that — or just not ready.

📷 *Jay looking off, unresolved*

[CTA]
Tomorrow I'm forcing myself to put it in front of four strangers. I'll tell you what happens either way. Follow if you want to see me actually ship something.

📷 *Jay reaching for phone, about to post*
