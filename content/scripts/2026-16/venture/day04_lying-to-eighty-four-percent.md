## Voiceover

[Hook — 0:00-0:04]
Ten days ago I shipped a product that was lying to eighty-four percent of the people who trusted it.

📷 *Screen recording: app with RED flags EVERYWHERE — visual chaos, 3+ cuts in first 3 sec*

[Problem]
The app was supposed to read a lease and flag the risky clauses. Normal stuff. Stuff landlords hope you don't catch.

📷 *Screen recording: lease text being scanned, flags appearing rapidly*

[Problem — the confession]
I ran it on ninety-nine test leases to see how it did. Eighty-four of them came back with the wrong answers.

📷 *Stats screen: 99 leases → 84 wrong → red bar chart*

[Breakthrough setup]
And here's the worst part — it wasn't breaking. It was confidently giving people wrong answers. Flagging fine clauses as dangerous. Missing real traps.

📷 *Close-up: one flagged clause that's actually fine, then a real trap going unflagged*

[Fix — the one change]
I spent two days rewriting the scoring rules from scratch. More specific prompts. More test cases. No more letting the AI guess.

📷 *Screen: code editor with rules being rewritten, then re-running the test*

[Fix — the result]
Ran it again. Eighty-four percent wrong dropped to eight.

📷 *Stats screen: 84% → 8% with green number*

[CTA]
Tomorrow I'm showing you the five things I hated about my own app after I finally used it like a real person. Follow to see what I broke.

📷 *Jay at laptop, slightly drained but focused*
