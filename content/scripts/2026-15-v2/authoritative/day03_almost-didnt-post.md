**Blueprint:** @chris.raroque — "app got hacked" — 150K views

## Voiceover

And I'm not gonna lie I almost didn't put it out there because I kept finding reasons to wait.

📷 *Jay staring at his phone, thumb hovering over "Post" button*

Like I had the app built. I had the content ready. And I just sat there for days telling myself it wasn't ready yet. That I needed one more feature. One more edit. One more review.

📷 *Screen showing a notes app with a list of "things to fix before posting"*

[beat] But it was never about the app not being ready. It was about me not being ready for people to tell me it's trash.

📷 *Jay looking directly at camera*

And here's what I realized — the fear doesn't go away. I still get nervous every time I post something. I still think about people judging me. The difference is I post it anyway now.

📷 *Screen recording of actually hitting "publish" or "post"*

[laid back here] And the thing nobody tells you is that most people don't even see it. And the ones who do? Most of them are rooting for you.

Follow me because I'm still figuring this out and I think that's kind of the point.

## Production Layers

- 🎤 **Spoken Hook:** "And I'm not gonna lie I almost didn't put it out there because I kept finding reasons to wait."
- 📷 **Visual Hook:** Thumb hovering over "Post" button — close-up, tense
- ✏️ **Written Hook:** "The app was ready. I wasn't."
- 🔊 **Audio Hook:** Quiet. Heartbeat barely audible.
- 🔁 **Loop:** "almost didn't put it out" → "post it anyway" → the fear resolves into action

## Delivery Notes
- "It was about me not being ready for people to tell me it's trash" — the real confession. Say it like you're admitting it for the first time.
- "Most people don't even see it" — slight laugh. The fear is bigger than the reality.
- The energy should shift from anxious to quietly confident by the end.
