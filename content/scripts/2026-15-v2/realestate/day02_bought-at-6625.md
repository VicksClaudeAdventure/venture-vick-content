## Voiceover

Everyone told me to wait for lower mortgage rates but I bought my house at 6.625% and I genuinely don't regret it.

📷 *Jay showing the rate on mortgage documents or app*

So right now rates are sitting at about 6.46%. Fannie Mae says they'll drop to 5.7% by December. And everyone's saying "just wait, rates are gonna come down."

📷 *News headline or Fannie Mae quote on screen*

But here's what happens when rates drop — every buyer who was waiting comes flooding back. More competition. Bidding wars. Sellers raise prices.

📷 *Simple graphic: more buyers → higher prices*

So you might save a hundred bucks a month on the mortgage but pay twenty thousand more for the house. That math doesn't work.

[beat] I locked in at 6.625%. Nobody was bidding against me. When rates hit low fives, I'll refinance and the payment drops. But the price I paid? That's locked in forever.

📷 *Jay at the actual house*

The rate is temporary. The price is permanent. Save this if you're debating whether to buy right now.
