## Voiceover

Me and two of my friends bought a house together in Philly and literally everyone told us it was a terrible idea.

📷 *Photo or video of the actual house — exterior shot*

Like "you're gonna ruin your friendships." "What happens when someone wants to sell?" "Three people on one mortgage is insane." We heard it all.

📷 *Jay reacting to the comments, slight eye roll*

And honestly? They weren't completely wrong. Buying a house with friends is complicated. Three names on a mortgage, three opinions on every renovation decision, three different financial situations.

📷 *The three friends together at the house, or the closing paperwork*

[beat] But here's why we did it anyway. None of us could afford a property alone. Together we could. And we'd rather own something complicated than rent something simple.

📷 *Inside the house — renovation in progress or completed*

The house is bought. The renovation is happening. And so far? Nobody's ruined any friendships.

Tomorrow I'm showing you why I bought at 6.625% when everyone said to wait. Follow if you're curious.

## Production Layers

## Delivery Notes
- The quotes from doubters should be said with slight amusement, not bitterness
- "They weren't completely wrong" — Jay acknowledges the risk. Honest, not defensive.
- "Rent something simple" vs "own something complicated" — that's the line. Let it land.
