## Voiceover

I put my app in front of real people for the first time and I was genuinely terrified the entire time.

📷 *Over-shoulder shot of someone using the app on their phone, Jay watching*

Not friends, not family — actual strangers. I just sat there watching them try to upload a lease and use the results.

📷 *Close-up of user's hands tapping through the app*

One person uploaded their lease and the results came back in twenty-nine seconds. They nodded. That felt amazing.

📷 *Jay's relieved face*

Another person couldn't figure out the upload button. My stomach literally dropped.

📷 *Jay wincing*

[beat] You build something in your room for weeks and you know every screen, every button, every flow. Then someone touches it for the first time and you realize they don't think like you at all.

Some people loved it. Some were confused. But nobody was lying to make me feel better and that's the most valuable thing you can get as a builder.

I'm documenting everything about building this app. Follow if you're curious what happens next.

## Production Layers

- 🎤 **Spoken Hook:** "I put my app in front of real people for the first time and I was genuinely terrified the entire time."
- 📷 **Visual Hook:** Over-shoulder of someone using the app — we see the user, not Jay
- ✏️ **Written Hook:** "First time someone else used it."
- 🔊 **Audio Hook:** Subtle heartbeat or pulse sound, barely audible
- 🔁 **Loop:** "terrified" at open → "most valuable thing" at close — fear becomes growth

## Delivery Notes
- "My stomach literally dropped" — flat, not dramatic. Understated is more real.
- The nodding moment vs. the confused moment should feel like a real emotional swing
- End reflective, not resolved. Jay's still processing this.
