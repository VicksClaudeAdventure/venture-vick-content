# VB4 — People Have a Lot to Say About How I Spend My Money

**Series:** V-Bucks
**Hook category:** Question | **Hook dimension:** Status | **Video angle:** Myth Bust
**Word count:** 147
**WPM estimate:** 171
**Hook word count:** 13

**Blueprint:** @meshtimes — "Side projects after work who" — 182K views
**Borrowed:** Identity-tag energy, community belonging, "who else deals with this" format
**Jay's fill:** Jay's actual experience being judged for investing in content equipment, rental property, and his startup

---

## Voiceover

I buy something for my side hustle and people act like I'm wasting my entire life savings. Does this happen to anyone else?

📷 *Jay holding up a piece of content equipment — ring light, camera, whatever*

Like I got a new camera for content and someone literally said "must be nice." I invested in equipment for my rental property — "why are you spending money on that?" I paid for a domain name for my app — twelve dollars — and someone had an opinion.

📷 *Quick montage of the items people judged*

[beat] Here's what I've noticed — people who comment on how you spend your money are telling you way more about their relationship with money than yours.

📷 *Jay talking directly to camera*

Someone who's comfortable with their finances doesn't track what you buy. Someone who's stressed about money notices everything.

[laid back here] So I stopped explaining my purchases to people who aren't paying my bills. And honestly that was one of the best financial decisions I've made.

Comment "my money" if you deal with this.

## Production Layers

- 🎤 **Spoken Hook:** "I buy something for my side hustle and people act like I'm wasting my entire life savings."

- The insight about other people's relationship with money should feel like a realization, not a lecture
- "My money" comment hook — engagement play
