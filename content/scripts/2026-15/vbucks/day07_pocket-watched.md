## Script

Why do people care so much about how you spend YOUR money?

You buy a nice pair of shoes — "must be nice." You invest in equipment for your side hustle — "that's a waste." You treat yourself to dinner — "I thought you were saving."

It's called being pocket watched. And it happens most when you're trying to level up.

Here's the truth — people who comment on your spending are telling you about THEIR relationship with money, not yours.

Someone who's comfortable financially doesn't track what you buy. Someone who's stressed about money notices everything you spend.

The fix isn't hiding your purchases. The fix is understanding that their reaction is about them.

[slow down here] Spend on what matters to you. Save for what matters to you. And stop explaining your money to people who aren't paying your bills.

Comment "my money" if you needed this.

## Delivery Notes
- The three examples at the top ("must be nice," "that's a waste," "I thought you were saving") should be said in a mocking voice. Quick impressions.
- "THEIR relationship with money, not yours" — emphasis on "their."
- The closer is a statement of independence. Confident, not defensive.
- Comment hook CTA — this one is designed for engagement.
