## Script

$139. Two adults. Three kids. 30 days. Here's the grocery list.

A family on Reddit broke down exactly how they feed five people for under $140 a month. 836 people commented. Most couldn't believe it.

The base: rice, beans, oats, eggs, frozen vegetables, whole chickens, bananas, peanut butter, bread, and canned tomatoes.

No name brand. No prepackaged meals. No snacks that cost $6 a bag.

They meal prep on Sunday. Bulk cook rice and beans for the week. Stretch one chicken across three meals — roast, soup, tacos.

The math — that's 93 cents per person per meal.

Is it glamorous? No. Is it sustainable when money is tight? Absolutely.

[slow down here] You don't need to spend $800 a month on groceries. You need a plan and a list.

Save this for the next time the grocery bill feels impossible.

## Delivery Notes
- Rattle off the grocery list quickly. The speed shows how simple it is.
- "93 cents per person per meal" — slow down. That's the number that stops scrolling.
- Tone is practical, not preachy. No shame. Just "here's how it's done."
