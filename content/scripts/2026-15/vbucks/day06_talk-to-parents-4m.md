# VB6 — Talk to Your Parents About Their Finances Before It's Too Late

**Series:** V-Bucks
**Hook category:** Story
**Word count:** 152
**WPM estimate:** 178
**Hook word count:** 13
**Weekly insights applied:** #2 (front-load), #3 (anger/injustice)

---

## Script

Someone's dad got scammed out of $4 million in 7 months. The family had no idea.

FBI got involved. IRS got involved. The money? Gone.

He posted on Reddit and said one thing — "talk to your parents about their finances."

Here's why this keeps happening. Scammers target people over 55 because they have savings, they're less familiar with digital fraud, and they're too embarrassed to tell their kids.

Your parents won't bring it up. You have to.

Three things to ask — Do you have someone managing your money? Has anyone contacted you about an investment opportunity? Do you know what a phishing email looks like?

[slow down here] It's an uncomfortable conversation. Having it once could save everything they've built.

$4 million. 7 months. No warning.

Send this to someone who hasn't had this conversation yet.

---

## Production Layers

- **Spoken Hook:** "Someone's dad got scammed out of $4 million in 7 months. The family had no idea."
- **Visual Hook:** Phone screen showing a bank account with rapidly decreasing balance animation.
- **Audio Hook:** Tense, minimal. Piano note on "$4 million."
- **Text Hook:** "$4,000,000 gone in 7 months" — the number hits harder written out.
- **Loop:** Opens with "$4 million in 7 months" → ends with the same line — the repetition is the warning.

## Delivery Notes
- "FBI got involved. IRS got involved." — rapid fire. Two short punches.
- "The money? Gone." — flat delivery. Don't dramatize it. The fact IS the drama.
- The three questions should be spoken like a checklist. Clear, direct, actionable.
- "It's an uncomfortable conversation" — softer here. You're giving permission.
