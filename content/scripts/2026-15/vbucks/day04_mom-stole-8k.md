## Script

11 years ago, his mom took his credit card "to keep it safe." Last week he found $8,000 in charges.

He was 18. Opened a Capital One card with a $300 limit. His mom said she'd pay it off and cancel it. He never saw the card again.

11 years later, he checks his credit report. That card? Still open. $8,000 balance. Collections calls. His credit score — destroyed.

Here's what nobody teaches you at 18 — when someone is an authorized user on your card, every charge is LEGALLY yours. Even if you didn't make them. Even if it was your mom.

You can dispute unauthorized charges, but the statute of limitations varies by state. Some give you 2 years. Some give you 6.

[slow down here] Don't give anyone your card. Not your mom. Not your best friend. Nobody.

Send this to someone who needs to hear it.

## Delivery Notes
- Third person framing ("his mom") — this is someone else's story from Reddit. Don't claim it.
- "LEGALLY yours" — hit that word hard. It's the gut punch.
- The CTA is a tag-a-friend. This is shareable content — people know someone in this situation.
