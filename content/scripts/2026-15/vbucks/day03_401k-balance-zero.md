## Script

Someone logged into their old 401K from a previous job. The balance was zero. No warning. No email. Nothing.

This happens more than you think.

When you leave a job and your 401K has less than $5,000, your old employer can legally move it without telling you. It's called a forced rollover. They dump it into a default IRA — that's an individual retirement account — usually with high fees and bad investments.

If it's under $1,000, they can straight up mail you a check. Which gets taxed. Plus a 10% penalty if you're under 59 and a half.

Your money. Moved without your permission. Taxed without your consent.

[this is the punchline — land it] The fix takes 5 minutes. Log into every old 401K you have. Right now. Roll it into an IRA you control before someone else decides for you.

Save this. Then go check your accounts.
