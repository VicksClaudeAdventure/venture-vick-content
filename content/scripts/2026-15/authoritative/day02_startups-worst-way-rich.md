# A2 — Founding a Startup Is the Worst Way to Get Rich

**Series:** Authoritative
**Hook category:** Bold take
**Word count:** 150
**WPM estimate:** 176
**Hook word count:** 12
**Weekly insights applied:** #2 (front-load)

---

## Script

If your only goal is to get rich, don't start a startup. Seriously.

90% of startups fail. Of the 10% that survive, most founders make less than they would have at a corporate job. The median startup founder salary is $0 for the first two years.

You know what actually makes people rich? Boring stuff. Real estate. Consulting. Buying an existing business. Sales jobs with uncapped commission.

A 265-comment thread on Reddit broke this down — the people who got wealthy didn't raise venture capital. They bought a laundromat. They started a cleaning company. They became the best salesperson at a mid-size company.

So why do I run a startup? Because I'm not doing it to get rich. I'm doing it to build something that's MINE.

[this is the punchline — land it] Build for ownership, not for money. The money follows.

Follow for the part of entrepreneurship nobody posts about.

## Delivery Notes
- Open with a slight pause after "seriously." It's a bold claim. Let it sit.
- The boring list (laundromat, cleaning company, salesperson) should be spoken with respect, not mockery.
- "Because I'm not doing it to get rich" — switch from teacher to personal. Jay is talking about himself now.
- The closer is philosophical. Earned authority from someone living it.
