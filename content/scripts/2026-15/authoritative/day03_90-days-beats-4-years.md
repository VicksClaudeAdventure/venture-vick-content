# A3 — 90 Days of Doing Beats 4 Years of Reading

## Script

I learned more in 90 days of building than I did in 4 years of reading about it.

I read every startup book. Listened to every podcast. Followed every founder on Twitter. I thought I was prepared.

I wasn't even close.

In 90 days of actually building, here's what the books never taught me — your first users don't care about your features. They care if it works. Your pricing is always wrong the first time. And the hardest part isn't coding — it's telling people your thing exists.

Four years of consuming content gave me confidence. Ninety days of building gave me COMPETENCE. Those are not the same thing.

[slow down here] Stop reading about starting. Start starting. The lessons only show up when you're in it.

You don't need another book. You need 90 days of doing the thing.

Follow for what those 90 days actually looked like.

## Production Layers

- **Spoken Hook:** "I learned more in 90 days of building than I did in 4 years of reading about it."
- **Visual Hook:** Stack of startup books being pushed aside, laptop opening.
- **Audio Hook:** Page-turning sound, then keyboard clicks replacing it.
- **Text Hook:** "Confidence =/= Competence"
- **Loop:** Opens with "4 years of reading" → ends with "you need 90 days" — consumption vs. creation.

## Delivery Notes
- "I wasn't even close." — deliver flat. Honest, not dramatic.
- The three lessons (users don't care about features, pricing is wrong, telling people is hard) should be rapid fire. Each one stings.
- "COMPETENCE" — capitalize verbally. It's the punchline word.
- The closer is a permission slip. Warm but firm. "You're ready. Go."
