## Script

Buyers are disguising themselves as renters to get inside your rental property.

A landlord posted about this on Reddit — multiple people showed up to "view the rental," asked detailed questions about the foundation, the roof, the property tax, the homeowner fees. Questions renters never ask.

Turns out they were investors scouting the property to make a purchase offer.

Why does this matter? Because they're getting a private tour of your property under false pretenses. They see every flaw. Every repair you haven't done. Then they lowball you with an offer based on what they found.

How to spot it — real renters ask about lease terms, parking, and move-in dates. Fake renters ask about the foundation, square footage, and comparable sales.

[slow down here] If someone asks about comps during a rental viewing — they're not there to rent.

Next video: how I screen tenants to avoid exactly this. Follow so you see it.
