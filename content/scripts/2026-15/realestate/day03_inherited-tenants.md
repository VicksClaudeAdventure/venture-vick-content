## Script

A landlord bought a property. The tenants refuse to sign his lease.

They claim the previous owner made verbal agreements — lower rent, no pet fees, flexible move-out. None of it written down.

Here's what new landlords don't know — in most states, verbal agreements from a previous owner DO carry some legal weight. You can't just throw them out.

But verbal agreements are almost impossible to enforce without a witness or written proof.

So what do you do? Two options.

Option one — negotiate. Offer a small discount to sign YOUR lease with YOUR terms. Most tenants take it.

Option two — cash for keys. Pay them to leave voluntarily. Cheaper than eviction.

[slow down here] Never buy a property without checking the existing lease situation FIRST.

Save this before you buy an investment property.
