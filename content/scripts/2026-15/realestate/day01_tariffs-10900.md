## Script

Your first house just got $10,900 more expensive. And you didn't do anything wrong.

New tariffs on building materials — lumber, steel, copper, cabinets — are adding almost $11,000 to the cost of every new home built in the US. That's according to the National Association of Home Builders.

But it gets worse. Higher building costs mean fewer homes get built. Estimates say 450,000 fewer new homes by 2030. Less supply. Same demand. Prices go up for EVERYONE — not just new builds.

If you're trying to buy your first property before 30, the math just got harder.

Mortgage rates are sitting at 6.46% right now. Fannie Mae says they'll drop to 5.7% by December. But even if rates fall, the homes themselves cost more.

[slow down here] Nobody's coming to fix this for first-time buyers. You have to plan around it.

Next video: should you wait for 5.7% rates or buy now? Follow so you see the math.
