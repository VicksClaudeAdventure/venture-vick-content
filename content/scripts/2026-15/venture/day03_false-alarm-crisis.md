## Script

My AI was flagging things wrong 84% of the time.

I built an app that reads your lease and flags risky clauses. Sounds great — until the AI started flagging EVERYTHING. Normal lines. Standard terms. Stuff that was completely fine.

84% false positive rate. That means if it flagged 10 things, 8 of them were wrong.

The product was useless.

I sat there staring at the results thinking — did I just waste two weeks?

So I rebuilt the entire scoring system. Rewrote the rules. Tested it against 99 different leases.

[slow down here] Got the false positive rate down to 8%.

From 84% broken to 8% accurate. Same product. Same AI. Better instructions.

That's the lesson — the AI is only as good as what you tell it to do.

Next video: I tested it myself and demanded 5 changes. That's when it became MINE. Follow so you see it.

## Production Layers

- **Spoken Hook:** "My AI was flagging things wrong 84% of the time."
- **Visual Hook:** Screen recording showing the app with red flags everywhere — visual overload of false positives.
- **Audio Hook:** Error/alert sound on the hook, then quiet tension.
- **Text Hook:** "84% WRONG" in red, large font. Transitions to "8%" in green.
- **Loop:** Opens with "84% wrong" → ends with "8% accurate" — the transformation is the loop.

## Delivery Notes
- Hook should sound like a confession. Embarrassed, not dramatic.
- "84%" and "8%" are the two numbers that anchor this script. Hit both clearly.
- "Did I just waste two weeks?" — genuine question, not rhetorical performance.
- The pivot to "better instructions" should feel like relief, not bragging.
