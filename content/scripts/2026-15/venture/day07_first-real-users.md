# V7 — First Real Users: Watching Strangers Use Your Thing

**Series:** Venture
**Hook category:** Insider reveal
**Word count:** 150
**WPM estimate:** 175
**Hook word count:** 12
**Weekly insights applied:** #1 (vulnerability), #2 (front-load), #4 (show the product)

---

## Script

Nobody tells you the scariest part of building an app is watching someone else use it.

I put RentShield in front of real people. Not friends. Not family. Strangers.

And I sat there. Watching.

One person uploaded their lease and the results loaded in 29 seconds. They nodded. That felt good.

Another person got confused by the upload button. My stomach dropped.

You build something in your room for weeks. You KNOW every screen, every button, every flow. Then someone touches it for the first time and you realize — they don't think like you.

Some people loved it. Some were confused. Nobody was lying to make me feel better.

[slow down here] That honesty is the most valuable thing a builder can get. And the hardest to sit through.

Next video: Week 3 numbers are in. 4 users. 6 analyses. Zero paying customers. Follow so you see what happened.

---

## Production Layers

- **Spoken Hook:** "Nobody tells you the scariest part of building an app is watching someone else use it."
- **Visual Hook:** Over-the-shoulder shot of someone using an app on their phone. Jay watching from behind, slightly tense.
- **Audio Hook:** Subtle heartbeat sound effect, barely audible.
- **Text Hook:** "The part nobody warns you about."
- **Loop:** Opens with "watching someone else use it" → ends with "hardest to sit through" — same experience, deeper understanding.

## Delivery Notes
- "And I sat there. Watching." — long pause between sentences. Let the viewer feel it.
- "My stomach dropped." — say it flat, not dramatic. Understated hits harder.
- The ending should feel reflective. You're not over it yet.
