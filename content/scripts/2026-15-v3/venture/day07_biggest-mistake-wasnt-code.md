## Voiceover

The biggest mistake I made building my app had absolutely nothing to do with the code.

📷 *Jay looking at RentShield, then looking at his phone — zero notifications*

So the app works. People who use it actually like it. But after three weeks I had four users and zero revenue because I spent all my time building and zero time telling people it exists.

📷 *Analytics showing flat traffic line*

I thought if I built something good enough people would just find it. That's not how it works. Nobody's browsing the internet looking for your app. You have to put yourself out there.

📷 *Jay setting up camera, starting to film content*

[beat] So I made a decision — stop building, start creating content. Document the whole journey and let the audience come to the product instead of the other way around.

📷 *Phone showing Instagram DM — brand deal offer*

And here's the part that surprised me — within weeks of posting consistently, a brand DM'd me on Instagram offering two hundred and fifty dollars for a video. I didn't pitch them. They found ME through the content.

📷 *Recovery tracker: $34,776.15 → $34,526.15*

The product was ready. I just needed people to see it. And content was the answer I didn't want to hear.

I'm documenting everything about building this app, follow if you're curious what happens next.
