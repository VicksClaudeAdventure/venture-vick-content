## Voiceover

Here's exactly every dollar three twenty-four-year-olds have put into a house in Philly and what it's worth now.

📷 *The house exterior → transition to spreadsheet*

So let's start with the purchase. $345,000. Ten percent down which was $34,500 split three ways — that's about $11,500 each out of pocket. Plus closing costs.

📷 *Purchase breakdown on screen*

Then the renovation. We budgeted twenty K and spent forty-five. Full bathroom gut, new roof, refinished all the hardwood, painted every wall ourselves, new fixtures everywhere.

📷 *Before/after transformation shots*

Monthly mortgage is $2,649 split three ways. Add insurance, taxes, and maintenance and each person pays under a thousand a month to own a house in Philadelphia.

📷 *Monthly cost breakdown per person*

[beat] And here's the number everyone wants to know — the house is worth about $385,000 now. We paid $345,000. That's roughly forty thousand dollars in equity in under two years.

📷 *Purchase price vs current value on screen*

We spent about $80K total between the down payment and the renovation. We've built $40K in equity. And we each pay less than a thousand a month. That's the math.

Save this if you've ever thought about buying property with friends.
