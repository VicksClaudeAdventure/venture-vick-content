## Voiceover

$345,000 house, three twenty-four-year-olds on the mortgage, and every single person told us not to do it.

📷 *Exterior of the actual house in Philly*

So in the summer of 2024 me and two of my friends went in on a four-bed, one-and-a-half-bath in Philly. Ten percent down, all three names on the mortgage, $2,649 a month.

📷 *Mortgage documents or closing paperwork*

And I mean EVERYONE had something to say. Family said we'd ruin our friendships. Coworkers said we were too young. Other investors said three people on one mortgage is insane.

📷 *Jay reacting to the criticism — slight eye roll*

[beat] And honestly they weren't totally wrong. Three people means three opinions on every decision. The ownership split is 37.5, 37.5, and 25 so even the split isn't equal. It's complicated.

📷 *Diagram showing the ownership split*

But here's why we did it — none of us could buy a house alone in Philly at twenty-four. Together we could. And I'd rather own something complicated than rent something simple for the rest of my twenties.

📷 *Inside the house — renovated, looking good*

Almost two years later, still friends, house is worth about forty thousand more than what we paid. I'll take it.

Tomorrow I'm showing you how badly we blew our renovation budget. Follow if you're curious.
