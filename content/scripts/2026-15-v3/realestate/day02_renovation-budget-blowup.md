## Voiceover

We budgeted twenty thousand dollars for our renovation and I'm honestly embarrassed to tell you what we actually spent.

📷 *Before photo of the house — rough shape*

So when we bought the house we knew it needed work. We said twenty K, maybe twenty-five max. That felt reasonable. New paint, some fixtures, refinish the floors. Easy.

📷 *The original renovation budget on paper or spreadsheet*

Then we opened up the bathroom. What was supposed to be a quick patch turned into a full gut job — we found a leak, had to tear everything out. New tile, new fixtures, new plumbing. That one room ate through most of the budget.

📷 *Bathroom mid-demolition — the disaster*

[beat] Then the dryer broke. Then a gutter ripped off in a storm. Then we found humidity issues in the basement nobody told us about. Every week it was something else.

📷 *Montage of problems stacking up*

Final number? Forty-five thousand dollars. My friend literally looked at me and said "bro, you said twenty K."

📷 *After photos — the house looking incredible*

He's right. But the house is beautiful now. We painted every wall ourselves, refinished every hardwood floor, new roof, new everything. Forty-five K over two years. Worth it.

Save this before you budget a renovation.
