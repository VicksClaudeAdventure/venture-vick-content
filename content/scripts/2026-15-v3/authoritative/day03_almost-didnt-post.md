# A3 — The Thing That Almost Stopped Me from Putting Any of This Out There

**Series:** Authoritative
**Hook category:** Contradiction | **Hook dimension:** Risk | **Video angle:** Transformation
**Word count:** 149
**WPM estimate:** 173
**Hook word count:** 14

**Blueprint:** @chris.raroque — "Number one mistake I made with my last app" — TikTok — 102K views
**Borrowed hook:** "Number one mistake I made with my last app was not adding Google and Apple login on day one."
**Hook mechanism:** MISTAKE CONFESSION + META — "almost made" = still in the danger zone, "afraid of what people would say" = universal fear
**Adapted hook:** "The number one mistake I almost made was never putting any of this out there because I was afraid of what people would say"
**Borrowed structure:** mistake identification → what it would have cost → the fix → lesson
**Jay's fill:** Jay's fear of launching RentShield and posting content

---

## Voiceover

The number one mistake I almost made was never putting any of this out there because I was afraid of what people would say.

📷 *Jay staring at his phone, thumb hovering over post button*

Like I had the app built, I had content ideas, I had stuff I genuinely wanted to share. And I sat on all of it for days because I kept imagining people going "who does this guy think he is."

📷 *Notes app showing drafted posts that were never posted*

[beat] And here's what I realized — the fear never goes away. I still get nervous before I post something. I still think about people judging me. Every single time.

📷 *Jay looking at camera directly*

The difference is I post it anyway now. Because the thing nobody tells you is that most people don't even see it. And the ones who do? Way more of them are rooting for you than you think.

📷 *Notification sounds, comments appearing — positive ones*

I almost let the fear of strangers on the internet stop me from building something real. Don't make that mistake.

Follow me because I'm still figuring this out and I think that's kind of the point.

## Delivery Notes
- "Who does this guy think he is" — say it like it's playing in your head. Not performing it.
- "Most people don't even see it" — slight laugh. The fear is bigger than the reality.
- The shift from anxious to quietly confident should feel gradual, not sudden.
