## Voiceover

It took me four years of watching other people build businesses before I realized I was just watching.

📷 *Jay's phone showing podcast apps, YouTube history, bookshelf*

I read the books. I listened to every podcast on my commute. I followed every founder on Twitter. I genuinely thought all that consuming was preparing me for something.

📷 *Stack of startup books, earbuds, Twitter feed scrolling*

[beat] It was not. Because the stuff I learned in two weeks of actually building was completely different from everything I'd read about.

📷 *Jay at his laptop, building RentShield*

Like — your first users don't care about your features, they care if it works. Your pricing is wrong the first time no matter what. And the hardest part of building isn't the technical stuff, it's telling people your thing exists.

📷 *Quick cuts: user feedback, pricing page, social media post*

Four years of consuming gave me confidence. Two weeks of building gave me competence. And I can tell you from experience those are not the same thing.

Follow me because I'm still learning this stuff every single day.
