## Voiceover

Here's exactly how much my rental property costs me every single month even when it's making money.

📷 *The property exterior or listing photo*

So everyone sees the rent check come in and assumes that's profit. It's not even close. Here's where the money actually goes.

📷 *Cost breakdown appearing on screen*

Mortgage is [$ amount] a month. Property insurance is [$ amount]. Taxes broken down monthly are [$ amount]. And then there's the fun one — maintenance. Something breaks like every other month. Last month it was [specific thing] for [$ amount].

📷 *Photo of whatever broke*

[beat] Add it all up and the property costs [$ amount] a month to own before a single dollar of rent comes in.

📷 *Calculator showing the math — rent minus costs = actual margin*

The rent covers it but the gap between what you collect and what you actually keep is way smaller than people think. I'm not saying don't buy property — I'd do it again. I'm saying know the real number first.

Save this before you buy your first rental.
