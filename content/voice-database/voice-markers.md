# Voice Markers — Aggregated Patterns

This file extracts the verbal fingerprints present across Jay's mentor-reviewed scripts. Claude Code should match these patterns when writing in Jay's voice.

## Sentence Starters
- "See," (at the start of backstory / context sections)
- "And," (mid-script pivots)
- "But," (contradiction beats)
- "Because" (origin story transitions)
- "Here's how..." (hook framing for transformation stories)

## Casing and Punctuation
- Lowercase "i" in conversational flow ("when i was 14," "i'd say")
- Ellipses for emotional or pacing beats ("…")
- Periods used sparingly after product names for emphasis ("RentShield.")
- Not afraid of run-on sentences when they mirror spoken rhythm

## Shorthand and Slang
- Texting shorthand used naturally: "w" for "with," "nbd" for "no big deal"
- Pop culture references with air quotes ("gurus," "generational wealth")
- "Literally" used as emphasis, not literal ("literally illegal")

## Number Handling
- Specific-to-the-cent dollar amounts in hooks ($34,776.15, $2,649)
- Rounded-down contrasts for the small amounts that add up ($19 deliveries)
- Specific bed/bath + location pairings (4 bed 2 bath in Philadelphia)
- Numbers written as words for emotional weight ("thirty-four grand," "fifty grand")

## Direct Viewer Address
- "And I know what you're thinking..." — acknowledging viewer skepticism
- "I'd say we are on the right track" — casual confidence
- "Which is literally illegal" — aside / reaction
- Second person "you" used to reframe ("The spending that ruins you is never the big stuff")

## Rhythm Devices
- Repetition for cadence ("I just spent and spent and spent")
- Three-beat lists ("rushed me into it, kept my security deposit, tried to charge me another two grand")
- Short aphoristic turn lines ("It's the receipts you stop reading")

## Origin Story Anchors
- Immigration reference (moved from Brazil at 14) — available for Recovery and general content
- Consulting job past — available for Venture content
- Three-friend house purchase — available for Real Estate content
- "A few years back" / "two years ago" / "when i was 14" as time markers

## Vulnerability Moves
- Admission of not-knowing ("I have no idea how it happened")
- Admission of freezing / fear ("the first thing I did was freeze")
- Admission of contradictions ("you would think that would mean i would be frugal but I wasn't")
- Self-deprecation about money mistakes, renovation budget, etc.

## Forward Plan / Series Runway Phrases
- "Starting today I'm..."
- "Tomorrow I'm showing you..."
- "Follow along if you want to..."
- "Follow if you want the [mess / crash / next part]"

## CTA Pattern
- Risk-framed CTAs are the Jay signature:
  - "Follow along if you want to watch this work or crash"
  - "Follow if you want the financial mess"
  - "Follow if you want to watch the recovery"
- Never "follow for more tips" or other generic CTAs
- The CTA frames the series as a gamble the viewer gets to witness

## Anti-Patterns (What Jay Does NOT Sound Like)
- Corporate jargon ("leverage," "synergize," "optimize")
- Guru phrases ("I'm gonna show you the exact blueprint")
- Over-polished aphorisms that sound written, not spoken
- Round numbers when specific numbers are available
- "Let me tell you about..." / "Today I want to talk about..." openers
- "Guys" as direct address
- Emoji-style enthusiasm ("SUPER excited to share this!!")
- Generic follow CTAs ("follow for more")

## Rhythmic Benchmark
Read aloud, Jay's scripts have a conversational WPM of 160-185. Sentences are built for spoken delivery, not reading. Long sentences are OK when the rhythm carries; choppy short sentences OK when hitting a beat.
