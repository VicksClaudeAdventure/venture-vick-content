# Structural Patterns — Aggregated

This file extracts the structural skeletons present in Jay's mentor-reviewed scripts. Claude Code should use these as reference for HOW Jay builds a script, while also pulling from series-specific blueprint creators for format fit.

## The Core Jay Skeleton

Across all three mentor-reviewed scripts, a pattern holds:

1. **Hook** — specific + impossible + invites curiosity
2. **Rehook or origin pivot** — "See," / "Because" / "And I know what you're thinking"
3. **Backstory or context** — the personal moment that created the situation
4. **Escalation or specifics** — stacked details that ground the story
5. **Turn or reframe** — the lesson, the product, the payoff
6. **Tomorrow tease or forward plan** — series runway
7. **Risk-framed CTA** — "watch this work or crash" style

Not every script uses every beat. The flex points are Beats 3-5 depending on whether the video is confessional (Recovery), build-in-public (Venture), or transformation (Real Estate).

## Series-Specific Patterns

### Recovery Series Skeleton
1. Shocking financial hook (specific dollar amount + surprise framing)
2. "And I know what you're thinking" rehook
3. Immigration / backstory origin
4. Spending spiral / escalation
5. Reframe (death by small cuts, not big moves)
6. Aphoristic turn
7. Forward plan ("making every dollar back with...")

### Venture Series Skeleton
1. Status drop + contrarian choice ("walked away from X to build Y")
2. Vulnerability admission ("first thing I did was freeze")
3. Origin problem (the personal experience that created the need)
4. Escalation of wrongs (three-beat stack)
5. Generalization ("this isn't just me")
6. Product reveal (named, one-sentence description, framed as solution)
7. Tomorrow tease
8. Risk-framed CTA ("watch this work or crash")

### Real Estate Series Skeleton
1. Impossibility hook ("did X when I shouldn't have been able to")
2. "See," backstory pivot
3. Decision moment ("so we decided to try it")
4. "Fast forward to present day" time jump
5. Specific current state (property details, dollar amounts)
6. Forward outlook (market, next property)
7. Payoff reveal ("still friends," "$50K up")
8. Financial-mess-framed tomorrow tease and CTA

## Hook Mechanisms That Work

- **Specificity + contradiction** (Recovery): $34,776.15 + "on NOTHING"
- **Status + contradiction** (Venture): Big firm + "something nobody asked for"
- **Age + impossibility** (Real Estate): 24 years old + no job + bought a house

The unifying principle: every hook contains a contradiction the viewer cannot resolve without watching. If a viewer can guess what the video is about from the hook, the hook has failed.

## Backstory Pivot Patterns

- "See when i was [age]..." (personal history)
- "Because a few years back, I [event]..." (recent past)
- "See, two years ago my [friend/partner] and I..." (origin of a collaboration)

## Escalation Patterns

- Stacked wrongs (RentShield: rushed me → kept deposit → tried to charge more)
- Stacked spending habits (Recovery: $19 deliveries → bar rounds → impulse buys)
- Stacked specifics (Real Estate: 4 bed 2 bath → Philadelphia → $2,649 → $2,800 rent)

## Turn / Reframe Patterns

- **Aphoristic** (Recovery): "The spending that ruins you is never the big stuff. It's the receipts you stop reading."
- **Generalization** (Venture): "Most of the traps landlords put in leases aren't even legal."
- **Payoff summary** (Real Estate): "Two years in, still friends, the place is worth fifty grand more than we paid."

## Forward Plan Patterns

- "Starting today I'm [doing X, Y, Z]" (Recovery — lists the recovery tactics)
- "Tomorrow I'm showing you [specific next thing]" (Venture + Real Estate — direct series runway)

## CTA Patterns

All three scripts use risk-framed CTAs:
- "Follow along if you want to watch this work or crash"
- "Follow if you want the financial mess"
- Implicit in Recovery: the whole video IS the CTA to watch the recovery happen

The CTA always frames the series as a journey the viewer gets to witness, not a course the viewer gets to consume.

## Length Benchmarks (To Be Refined With Performance Data)

Mentor-reviewed scripts run approximately:
- Recovery 1 — ~150 words
- RentShield 1 — ~130 words
- 3 Friends 1 House — ~175 words

At Jay's 160-185 WPM target, these map to 45-65 seconds. Target for future scripts based on analytics feedback: 30-40 seconds. This is a tightening target, not a current benchmark.
