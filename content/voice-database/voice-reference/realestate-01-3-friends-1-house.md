# 3 Friends 1 House — "Bought a house at 24 with no job"

**Series:** Real Estate
**Status:** Voice Reference (pending film)
**Mentor Reviewed:** Yes
**Date Added:** 2026-04-16
**Performance Data:** Not yet filmed

---

## Full Script

Here's how i bought a house w my friends as a 24 year old with no job. See, two years ago my two friends and I were spending all day doom scrolling and seeing videos of so called called "gurus" real estate hacking, promising us "generational wealth" So we decided to try it out and give it a shot for ourselves. Fast forward to present day, we own a 4 bed 2 bath in Philadelphia where we pay $2,649 a month and are in the process of renting it out for over $2,800. We knew that there would be a learning curve in this leap of faith and as a result we decided to pick a property in one of the fastest growing areas in Philadelphia. This area has a generally young demographic featuring young professionals and college students with a pretty decent night life. We expect the market to keep growing and leading us to buy another property in Philadelphia. And Two years in, still friends, the place is worth fifty grand more than we paid, I'd say we are on the right track. Tomorrow I'm showing you how badly we blew our renovation budget. Follow if you want the financial mess.

---

## Voice Markers Present

- "Here's how..." as a hook framing
- Texting shorthand ("w" for "with")
- Age + impossibility ("as a 24 year old with no job")
- "See," as a sentence starter into backstory
- Cultural reference ("doom scrolling," "gurus," "generational wealth" in air quotes)
- "Fast forward to present day" as time-jump bridge
- Specific numbers ($2,649, $2,800, fifty grand)
- Conversational asides ("still friends")
- Casual confidence ("I'd say we are on the right track")
- Tomorrow tease with self-deprecation ("how badly we blew our renovation budget")
- Risk-framed CTA ("Follow if you want the financial mess")

## Structural Skeleton

1. **Hook** — the impossible claim ("bought a house at 24 with no job")
2. **Origin** — social media inspiration (the gurus, the doom scrolling)
3. **Decision** — "so we decided to try it out"
4. **Present state** — specific property, specific numbers, cash flow
5. **Justification** — why this neighborhood, market outlook
6. **Payoff** — two years in, up $50K, friendships intact
7. **Tomorrow tease** — renovation budget disaster
8. **Risk-framed CTA** — "follow if you want the financial mess"

## Hook Mechanism

Impossibility. "24 years old with no job" + "bought a house" should not go together. The viewer's immediate question is HOW, and the video has to resolve that question.

## Why This Script Works (Pre-Performance Hypothesis)

- Hook creates a financial impossibility the viewer wants resolved
- Three-friend ownership is novel — most real estate content is solo
- Specific numbers ground the story in reality
- Self-deprecating renovation tease extends viewer into next video

## Critique Notes (From Earlier Review)

- Hook promises to explain HOW the mortgage happened with no job, but the script never addresses mortgage qualification — this is the biggest unresolved tension
- Middle of the script (neighborhood justification) is a victory lap before viewers understand the mechanics
- Ending shifts tone from "here's how we did it" to "tomorrow I'll show you the mess" — works, but middle needs tightening
- Typo present: "so called called" (likely fixes in film)

## Borrowable Elements for Future Real Estate Scripts

- Impossibility hooks ("did X when I shouldn't have been able to")
- "See," backstory pivot
- "Fast forward to present day" as time jump
- Specific dollar amounts paired with the specific property (bed/bath count, location, rent vs. mortgage)
- "Still friends" / "still together" style parenthetical payoffs
- Renovation/money mess teases as natural cliffhangers
- Financial-mess-framed CTAs

## Pending Improvement Before Filming

The script needs to address the "no job mortgage" mechanic somewhere — either explicitly explain it briefly, OR reframe the hook so it doesn't promise an answer it doesn't deliver. This is a content-review item before this script should be filmed.
