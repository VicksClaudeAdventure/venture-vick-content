If inflation is bad, can deflation make everyone better off?
No, because while falling prices can increase that purchasing power at first, sustained deflation
can reduce the demand, increase the real debt burdens and weaken the economy.
If people expect prices to keep falling, they may delay spending.
For example, if you know the price will be cheaper tomorrow, why don't you buy it today?
Lord Demon reduces business revenue, which can lead to lower wages, less investments and job losses.
Deflation can increase the real value of debts, because income and price will keep falling
while debt stays the same.
Therefore, this makes repayments even harder, especially for highly indebted households or businesses.
These effects can reinforce each other, creating a cycle of falling prices and weak demand.
Also, we have a historical evidence, great depression and it shows how damaging this can be.
So, in conclusion, no, deflation doesn't always make everyone better off.
Sometimes the harm is more than just lower prices.
