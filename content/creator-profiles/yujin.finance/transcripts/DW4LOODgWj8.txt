Does printing money always cost inflation?
No, because printing money only leads to inflation
if it increases the spending relative to the economy's capacity.
But inflation depends whether that money actually leads to higher demand
if newly created money stays in bank reserves
or is saved rather than spent, the demand may not increase much.
And this happens after 2008 global financial crisis.
That time the central bank expanded their balance sheets
but inflation stayed relatively low due to weak demand and cautious lending.
So the outcome changes under different conditions.
If the economy is close to its full capacity,
many creation can support stronger spending.
So the demand can exceed supply and that leads to inflation.
Also, expectations matter.
If people expect higher inflation, they might spend sooner.
If you know that price can increase tomorrow, you probably buy it today.

So the key question is not just whether the money is created or not.
