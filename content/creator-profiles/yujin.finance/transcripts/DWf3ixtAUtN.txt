Can rapid growth increase risk of collapse?
Yes, because growth is cash.
Imagine on a coffee business.
In one year, I open 10 different locations.
The revenue growth, everything is successful.
But here's the problem.
Growth needs cash.
So now I need to buy more coffee beans inventory bills off.
Let suppliers wait for the payment.
Let customers pay later, reasonable growth.
Because unless they pay cash, I don't have money.
I need to buy more coffee machines and renovate the stores,
which is capital expenditure.
Most importantly, I need to borrow money to open new locations,
which leads to more deaths.
So even though sale is growing, cash is living everywhere.
It's possible that I might run out of cash even before I open a new store.
Sometimes business doesn't collapse because it's small.
It collapses because it grows too fast without enough cash.
So next time if someone tries to break you,
I'll open five new stores just last month.
Just say good luck, because we never know what's going to happen
when business is growing too fast.
