## Creator Profile: @yunghunid
**Platform:** TikTok
**Series assignment:** Venture
**Format tags:** S (solo confessional talking head) — serialized "Starting over at 32" arc
**Outliers analyzed:** 10 (9 usable English, 1 music-only)

### Recurring Hook Patterns
- **Pattern 1 (age + loss-stack identity hook):** Rigidly repeated tentpole. "Starting over at 32 with no job, no social life, and no clue what's next." / "Starting over at 32, with no job, no social life, and no clue what's next." / "Starting over a 32 with no job, no social life, no clue what's next." / "Starting over at 32 with no job and no friends left." — appears in 4+ videos. Identity + stacked losses ("no job, no social life, no clue") + implied stakes.
- **Pattern 2 (can't-afford + day counter):** A secondary tentpole that acts as a progress bar. "I can't afford this apartment anymore so this is day 5 with no job at 32." / "I can't afford this apart[ment] anymore so this is day 6 of no job at 32." — appears in 2+ videos. Scarcity confession + rising day number.
- **Pattern 3 (rupture-confession cold open):** Single-sentence emotional shock. "I lied to my mom for three years and she just found out and the way she reacted literally broke me." / "I lost my best friend because I awaited for them the text first." — appears in 2+ videos. These are his breakout moments inside the serialized arc.

### Script Structure
- **Typical structure:** Repeating hook → "let's rewind" / "You see..." backstory → specific scene this week (saw a friend's story, rejection email, selling a possession) → reframe ("maybe I'm not losing things, maybe I'm making space for what's next") → soft CTA ("follow to see my life after quitting my job").
- **Average length:** ~160–220 words (~45–60s).
- **Pacing notes:** Steady, low-energy, vulnerable. He never accelerates — the monotone IS the tone. Every video ends on a small forward step rather than a win.

### Storytelling Mechanics
- **Show vs tell:** Balanced. He tells the emotion but anchors it to one concrete specific — "sell a pair of Jordans my cousin got me for my birthday", "applied over a thousand jobs", "10 years of work experience", "my friend's story... closest friends at the park with their kids". The specific always has a number or a name.
- **Visuals:** Inferred — apartment interior, phone screen with rejection emails, LinkedIn, walking/driving POV, possessions being sold.
- **Emotional arc:** Loss → shame → small reframe → fragile hope. He never resolves, which is exactly why the series compounds.

### CTA Style
- **Typical approach:** Soft franchise CTA tied to the series identity. "Follow along and see my life after quitting." / "Follow to see my life after quitting my job." / "Fall[ow] to see my life out [after] quitting." Nearly every video ends with some variant of this exact phrase.

### What Makes Their Outliers Work
- The hook is a franchise: same "Starting over at 32" line across 4+ videos, letting viewers drop in anywhere without onboarding.
- He's willing to be the loser in the story — no flexing, no win-porn. The honesty is the edge in a feed full of hustle posturing.
- Numbers land every video — "10 years of work experience", "over a thousand jobs applied", "five months unemployed". Specificity makes the shame real.
