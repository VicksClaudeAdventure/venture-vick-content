Starting over a 32 with no job, no social life, no clue what's next.
Well, let's rewind. After three months of starting my job, I became number one of my team and
made the most money out of everyone. And honestly, that's when the burnout began. That was top
performer. The guy I could count on. The one leadership praised in every meeting. But when I try to
answer, who am I without this job? I was dumb because I had no idea who it was. I'd spent my
entire 20s becoming a workforce that it was all I knew, so I quit. Not because I failed, but
because I finally got honest with myself. I'd rather start over a 32 and figure out what I
actually want in life and know what I don't like. Fall to see my life out through quitting.
