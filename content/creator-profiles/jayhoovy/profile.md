### Script Structure

- **Typical structure:** Declarative premise → one concrete example/name-drop → principle extraction → contrarian reframe → implicit-permission ending (no hard CTA). Most videos read like 30-60 second slices of a longer podcast or founder panel rather than purpose-built short-form.
- **Average length:** 50-80 seconds for the business clips (14-22 lines).
- **Pacing notes:** Even, conversational, unhurried. Zero on-screen text gymnastics implied. He doesn't race — he trusts the idea density. Each sentence lands one concept.

### Storytelling/Teaching Mechanics

- **Credential-then-contrarian.** "I learned this at Goldman Sachs" → "and the secret is the opposite of what Goldman Sachs represents." Uses the institution to earn attention then subverts it.
- **Name-drop pattern-matching.** Teaches abstract lessons (build a brand bigger than yourself) by stacking concrete famous examples (Kylie, Fenty, Feastables, Goop). Viewer pattern-matches the principle themselves.
- **Permission to have a mission.** Patagonia clip reframes "do good AND do well" as the hill to die on. He's selling a worldview, not a tactic.
- **Vulnerability as differentiation.** Drops "they just need to fucking go to therapy" and "maybe that's extremely naive of me" — positions himself as a thinking, feeling founder, not a hustle bro.

### CTA Style

### What Makes Their Outliers Work

1. **Elite credential + anti-elite message.** Goldman Sachs guy telling you the Goldman Sachs path is not the path. Built-in cognitive dissonance = retention.
2. **Brand-name density.** Kylie, Rihanna, Mr. Beast, Gwyneth, Patagonia, Ivan Chouinard, Elon Musk. Famous names are free attention.
