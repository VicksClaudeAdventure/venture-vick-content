Something I've learned from posting about money
for the last couple of years
is that overly negative people are just desperate
to feel better about their situation or their choices.
Like when I talk about retiring at 40,
people are always like, well, you can't do that
because you can't even take the money out until 59 and a half.
They don't know anything about my situation
or how I'm setting myself up for retirement.
They just go straight to, well, you can't do that
because internally, they're upset that they can't do that.
Maybe they don't make enough, maybe they make a lot
and just have a spending problem,
but the bottom line is they're just angry
at their own circumstances.
Another one I see all the time
is people getting mad at how much people are investing.
It's not realistic to invest $100 every month.
No one has $100 every month.
And again, this just tells me
that they feel bad about their situation.
They wish they could invest $100 a month
and instead of taking education and inspiration from posts,
they decide to be negative towards them.
Because the truth is you can invest $1 a month
and that's better than nothing
and that's something to be proud of
and you're building a habit to hopefully invest more
in the future.
But if you decide to just be overly negative,
you're never gonna start at all.
Ultimately, everything that someone comments
is just a reflection of themselves
and how they feel about themselves.
I do have empathy for it all being overworked
and overwhelmed and feeling like you don't have enough
can lead you to be a very negative person
and seeing people online with hope and plans
for the future can be very triggering.
It's important to remember that we are all on our own journeys
and you never see the full picture of someone's journey
in a video on social media.
No one wins in the comparison game,
not the person you're comparing yourself to and not you.
I mean, I graduated with $100,000 in debt.
Everyone had more money than me.
I had two roommates, but I chose to use other people's success
as proof that I could be successful.
I didn't tear people down because I was feeling bad
about myself.
I don't get discouraged, I get inspired,
and I get resourceful.
I'm really scrappy and that's why I make plans so confidently
because I know if the stock market crash and goes down
for the next 40 years, I will be fine
because I will pivot, I will make it work.
That's the kind of attitude I want you guys to have.
