## Creator Profile: @micro.investing
**Platform:** TikTok
**Series assignment:** V-Bucks
**Format tags:** E (Educational), D (Documentary/Personal-journey)
**Outliers analyzed:** 9 (1 transcript was non-English/untranscribable)

### Recurring Hook Patterns
- **Pattern 1: Counterintuitive confession + "here's how it made sense"** — Example: "Everybody told me it was insane to sign up for a $1200 a month car payment, but here's how it made it make sense." — appears in 3 videos (the $1200/month car, the $52K Sienna paid-off, the "I don't just own stocks" list).
- **Pattern 2: Callout + permission to be wrong** — Example: "If you're a two-income household and still feel broke, this is why." and "If your paycheck is hitting your checking account first, I'm sorry, but you're doing it wrong." — appears in 2+ videos. Formula: IF [reader's situation], THEN [you're doing it wrong / here's why].
- **Pattern 3: Warning/prediction hook** — Example: "I'm going to warn you, by the end of this video, you're going to want a minivan." — promises a transformation by the end.
- **Pattern 4: Retrospective timeline opener** — Example: "When Amy and I met in 2020, our combined net worth was $50,000." — starts in the past with a specific number, builds a net-worth journey.

### Script Structure
- **Typical structure:** Hook (counterintuitive claim) → concession ("I know, I know, buying a new car is one of the worst...") → reveal of the underlying system ("we structured our finances...") → specific numbers/receipts ($6,000/month passive income) → principle/philosophy ("buy assets first, liabilities second") → soft CTA.
- **Average length:** 25–40 seconds / 150–250 words.
- **Pacing notes:** Tight, no wasted lines. Every sentence either raises a stakes flag or drops a number. He concedes the objection immediately ("I know, I know") which neutralizes the "actually" crowd before they can comment.

### Storytelling/Teaching Mechanics
- **Show vs tell:** Heavy on tell, but every abstract claim is anchored to a tangible asset (minivan, cold storage facility, cupcake bakery, whiskey distillery). The "weird stuff" list is the core mechanic — it makes alternative investing feel visual.
- **Visuals:** He himself + the van as a recurring prop. The minivan becomes a symbol for the whole financial philosophy.
- **Numbers/receipts:** Extremely specific and repeated. "$161/mo to $6,000/mo." "$52,000 Toyota Sienna." "9x return in just over a year." "$50K to $1M net worth." Numbers are always paired with timeframes.

### CTA Style
- **Typical approach:** Comment-gated lead magnets. Exact quotes: "comment, newsletter, and I'll send it right over to you"; "just comment playbook and I'll DM it over to you"; "If you want first access, comment, explore, or you can DM that to me." Also softer follow CTAs: "If you want to make financial decisions on your own terms, follow me to see how I do it every month."

### What Makes Their Outliers Work
- **Heretical financial takes that actually work out:** Each video takes a position financial Twitter would hate (buy a $1200/mo minivan, finance instead of paying cash) and then shows the math that makes it defensible. The tension between "everyone says no" and "here's my math" is the whole engine.
- **Asset vs liability framing as a repeatable mental model:** "Don't buy luxuries with your labor. Plan ahead and invest in assets and let them pay for your liabilities." This one-liner gets reused across multiple scripts as a unifying philosophy.
- **Alternative investing as identity bait:** The weird-portfolio list (cupcake bakery, whiskey distillery, mobile home park) is unique enough to be saveable/shareable on its own.
