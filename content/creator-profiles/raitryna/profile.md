## Creator Profile: @raitryna
**Platform:** TikTok
**Series assignment:** V-Bucks
**Format tags:** D (Documentary/vlog), T (Talking-head listicle), E (Educational)
**Outliers analyzed:** 10

### Recurring Hook Patterns
- **Pattern 1: Personal stakes / high-number flex hook** — Example: "I've been posting on TikTok every single day for the last eight months and not only has it changed my life financially But I've also been able to generate over 76 million views and gain over 200,000 followers" and "Just in case your parents didn't teach you about money, here's everything I did the day I turned 18 years old to set me up to win in life. And because I followed these rules, I now have a perfect credit in over a million dollar net worth at 26 years old."
- **Pattern 2: Announcement / urgency opener** — Example: "My manager just called me and told me to book the next flight to New York. Let's pack." and "The government is still shut down and I just booked a last-minute trip to New York. Let's see if I make it." — action is already underway.
- **Pattern 3: Mild-confession + emotional truth** — Example: "I like spending money, but I really love to save money, so here are three things I did this month to help me save hundreds without feeling like I sacrificed anything." and "One thing I refuse to do is pay $900 a year for this MX Platinum and not take advantage of all the benefits."
- **Pattern 4: Roleplay/character bit** — Example: "My mom's actually a menace because she just called me talking crazy saying she could beat me any game of chess." — personal relationship drama pulls you in.

### Script Structure
- **Typical structure (educational list):** Hook (personal flex/credential) → "starting off with number one" → 5–7 numbered tips delivered fast → closing philosophical line. Minimal/no CTA.
- **Typical structure (vlog):** Hook (something's happening NOW) → packing/prep montage → stakes/conflict ("flight delayed") → resolution → tag line ("please be kind to airport workers").
- **Average length:** 60–90 seconds / 350–500 words. List videos are rapid-fire.
- **Pacing notes:** Very dense on listicles — he stacks 6+ tips in under a minute. Zero throat-clearing. Each numbered tip has one clear sentence, one justification, sometimes a micro-joke, then next.

### Storytelling/Teaching Mechanics
- **Show vs tell:** Vlogs show everything (packing, airports, shopping for suits). Listicles are pure talking head with captions.
- **Visuals:** B-roll heavy for vlogs, minimal for lists. Credit card closeups for finance lists.
- **Numbers/receipts:** Specific and dropped casually. "$900/year Amex Platinum." "$600 hotel credit, $300 equinox, $200 airline." "$816 suit total, 20% off = $653." "Million dollar net worth at 26." Numbers are usually personal (his money, his credits, his purchases).

### CTA Style
- **Typical approach:** Often no explicit CTA on listicle videos — he ends on a bold philosophical line ("looks fucking matter"). On branded/sponsored content: soft product mention + "learn from my mistakes." On vlogs: a human request ("please be so kind to airport workers"). When he does CTA, it's specific: "If you want to practice with me, add me on Duolingo."

### What Makes Their Outliers Work
- **Unapologetic personality + receipts** — He curses, he flexes, he drops "looks fucking matter" — the voice is not sanitized and that's what separates him from typical finance creators. The credentials (million net worth, 200K followers, 76M views) let him get away with the swagger.
- **Life-happening-right-now vlogs** — the "my manager just called me" hook creates immediate momentum. The viewer is dragged into the present tense.
- **Personal rules as content** — his 18-year-old checklist, his Valentine's Day list, his suit purchase logic all convert lifestyle into a spreadsheet of tips.
