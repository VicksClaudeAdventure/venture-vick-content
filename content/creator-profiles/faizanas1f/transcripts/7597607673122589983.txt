A lot of people seem to believe that the reason I'm able to speak so quickly and fluently
in all of my videos is because I have so many cuts in them, and while this might be true
in theory, it's simply not the case in reality, and I'm going to prove that by doing today's
video in entirely one take without any cuts.
The reason I have so many cuts in my videos is to keep them visually engaging, not to make
it easier for me, but to make it easier for you.
People have short attention spans, and the moment they sense an ounce of monotony in any
video they watch, they will scroll away.
The opposite of monotony is movement, and this applies to both what you hear and what
you see.
I try to implement this in all of my videos.
Moreover, by keeping this visual engagement, I'm also making sure that you retain the
information I'm saying to you far better.
I see so many creators nowadays with talking head videos, and they have so much good information
in these videos, but they're speaking slowly and staying in the same place the entire
time.
This makes me more likely to be bored listening to you, and far less likely to actually
process any of the information that you're sharing to me.
Finally, I don't really script any of these videos.
I have a list of talking points that I want to get through, and I move around the room
as I'm getting through them, but I don't follow a specific word for word script.
Most of these videos are done entirely, extemporaneously, and this is a skill that I believe
everyone can and should have.
That is the entire basis of this account, and it's what I'm trying to teach you to do.
