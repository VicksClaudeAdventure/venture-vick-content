### Script Structure

- **Typical structure:** Hook (confession/question) → Self-interruption / sketch cut-in → Relatable pain point reframed as a "wait, but then..." moment → Scripture or analogy as the pivot → Comedic voice/character break (often a puppet/alter-ego/AI friend) → Landing message tying it to identity → Short payoff line ("That's really the most." / "Class is dismissed!" / "Yo, it's a bar.")
- **Average length:** 28-35 seconds (20-40 lines of dialogue).
- **Pacing notes:** Norm rapid-cuts between camera angles, voices, and characters. Nearly every beat has either a punchline, a physical bit, or a voice change. Tension is sustained by layering in multiple sub-hooks ("But did he really? Nope.") and self-deprecating asides.

### Storytelling/Teaching Mechanics

- **Self as failing protagonist.** Every video positions Norm as the sinner/doubter/failer, not the teacher. He earns authority by confessing first.
- **Absurd analogy as teaching vehicle.** Fluid dynamics for the Holy Spirit. "Holy Spirits cubed per second." ChatGPT as a lazy shortcut dramatized with a stuffed animal. The more ridiculous the analogy, the stickier the lesson.
- **Two-character dialogue.** Norm vs. an alter-ego, a puppet, a stuffed Jesus, or AI Jarvis. The other "character" always asks the dumb question the viewer is thinking.
- **Scripture drop as the twist.** The verse isn't the opening — it's the midpoint reveal that reframes everything above.
- **Gen-Z slang + theological content.** "Stacking cheddar," "plastered," "booning edge," "down bad," "the train ride," "bread of value." The contrast is the comedy.

### CTA Style

- Soft / post-credits, almost never a direct "follow." Examples:
  - "No, send this to a buddy who needs one for you." (DSp62_IjTv_)
  - "Follow and share with three friends." (DTQYR-lDeEf — delivered as a gag via a puppet)
  - "I know you're screaming for how to write an epically cinematic story, so comment KRP and I'll coach you how." (DTQYR-lDeEf)
  - Most videos end on a punchline instead of a CTA: "That's really the most." / "Anyways, class is dismissed!" / "Yo, it's a bar." / "He is risen. Let's go!"

### What Makes Their Outliers Work

1. **Confession authority.** He admits the thing the audience is embarrassed to admit (relapse, doubt, performativity, loving Christmas more than Christ). That self-exposure earns the right to teach.
2. **Sketch density.** Every 3-5 seconds there's a voice change, a cutaway, a puppet, or a meme overlay. Retention is engineered at the second-by-second level.
3. **The question-as-hook is always one the audience has privately wondered** but felt shame asking. He voices the shame and then answers it.
4. **High-concept reframes.** He turns a tired topic (Bible study, prayer, self-improvement) into a novel mechanism ("fluid dynamics," "three words," "renewing the mind = food for the soul").
5. **A stable identity loop.** Every script ends by reaffirming his mission ("serving God while making dope videos — cinematically, of course"). The brand compounds.
