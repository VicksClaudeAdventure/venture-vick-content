## Creator Profile: @keemccomb
**Platform:** TikTok
**Series assignment:** Venture
**Format tags:** D (doc POV vlog) + S (confessional pieces-to-camera)
**Outliers analyzed:** 10

### Recurring Hook Patterns
- **Pattern 1 (one-way flight + stakes):** "I just booked the one way to Alaska to live with 50 s[l]ed dogs and the winter in my hut." / "It feels so surreal that I really just dropped everything to move to Alaska in the middle of the winter for two to three months with 50 sled dogs." / "You can indeed literally just do shit. I booked a one-way to Alaska in the middle of the winter..." / "I'm $49,000 in debt, so I booked the one-way flight to Alaska." / "I'm $49,000 in debt, so I booked the one-way flight to Alaska, and I can't leave until I clear 20 of it." — appears in 5 videos. "Booked the one-way" is his signature phrase.
- **Pattern 2 (debt + geographic self-imposed constraint):** "I'm $49,000 in debt, so I booked the one-way flight to Alaska. Now I can't leave until I clear 20 of it." / "Today is day two, and the first deal is officially clear." — appears in 2+ videos. He stacks a number (debt) with a self-imposed rule (can't leave until X) — a progress bar disguised as a hook.
- **Pattern 3 (environmental awe-hook):** "It is currently 8 a.m. in Alaska, and this is outside." / "It is currently negative 22 degrees in the middle of Alaska..." / "Sometimes I forget that not too many people will drop everything they're doing in their life to move out to the middle of Alaska doing a winter and live in a year[t]." — appears in 3 videos. Uses the setting itself as the cold open.

### Script Structure
- **Typical structure:** Bold decision hook → "let me catch you up real quick" backstory → reveal of the constraint/goal → specific tactical reveal (brand deal math, day count) → progress update → tomorrow promise.
- **Average length:** ~180–250 words (~50–70s).
- **Pacing notes:** Fast, high-energy, lots of "look at this". Slows only when he breaks down the money math.

### Storytelling Mechanics
- **Show vs tell:** Extreme show. The yurt, the dogs, the frozen nostrils, the negative 22 thermometer — he narrates around what's physically on screen. When he does tell (the $49K debt, the brand-deal breakdown), the numbers are hyper-specific: "$8,000 deal... $1,000 each [to creators]... about 4K net... 35 videos per month."
- **Visuals:** Yurt interior, sled dogs, sunrise/sunset (4pm dark), snow walks, phone POV walking.
- **Emotional arc:** Awe → isolation → faith/discipline → progress. Always ends on forward motion ("See you tomorrow.").

### CTA Style
- **Typical approach:** Zero traditional CTA — replaced with a day-count cliffhanger. "Day one, zero out of $20,000 to go. I'll see you tomorrow." / "Day two, $4,000 out of $20,000. See you tomorrow." The daily promise IS the follow mechanic.

### What Makes Their Outliers Work
- Self-imposed geographic constraint is an incredible attention device — "I can't leave until I clear $20K" is a physical cliffhanger.
- Transparent brand-deal math — he shows the actual breakdown of an $8K deal, which almost no creator does. Rewards the audience for paying attention.
- The environment does half the work. Negative 22 and a yurt don't need a script.
