## Creator Profile: @coryjweigel
**Platform:** TikTok
**Series assignment:** Real Estate
**Format tags:** S (serialized origin story), D (documentary vlog)
**Outliers analyzed:** 1

### Recurring Hook Patterns
- **Pattern 1: Rhetorical-question origin hook** — Opens with a question framing himself as an unlikely underdog, immediately challenging the viewer's assumptions about who gets to own real estate/businesses. Example: "How does an average 23 year old dude buy a million dollar liquor store?" — appears in 1 of 1.
- **Pattern 2: Preemptive objection handling** — Second line kills the two obvious dismissals before the viewer can think them. Example: "And no, this place wasn't passed down through family. And my dad definitely wasn't rich enough to buy it for me." — this locks in viewers who were about to scroll.

### Script Structure
- **Typical structure:** (1) Unlikely-outcome question hook, (2) Kill the "he was handed it" objection, (3) Origin backstory — the bad years, the failed attempts (drop shipping, Amazon, agency, high-ticket course), (4) The pivot moment — "until I was crazy enough to..." (5) Reframe the hate — "people love to call me crazy for X, Y, Z," (6) Humility + vision — "lots of people think I have it figured out, I really don't," (7) CTA with a "this was just step one" tease.
- **Average length:** ~250 words / ~60-75 seconds.
- **Pacing notes:** Builds from defeat to defiance. Uses list-of-three ("drop shipping, Amazon stores, starting a marketing agency") to compress multiple failures into one breath. Switches from past-tense struggle to present-tense conviction right at the pivot.

### Storytelling/Teaching Mechanics
- **"People call me crazy for X, Y, Z" rule-of-three sacrifice list** — compresses the real cost of the bet into rapid-fire specifics ("leaving everyone I knew behind, sacrificing spending time doing the things I actually love, sit in a liquor store every day in pretty much the middle of nowhere").
- **Vulnerable flex:** "lots of people think I have it all figured out, but I really don't." Disarms the viewer and earns trust.
- **"Step one" framing:** positions the entire video as a pilot episode for a much bigger saga.

### CTA Style
- "So make sure you hit that follow. Because this was all just step one. Who knows what's up next."
- CTA is wrapped inside a narrative device — the follow is the only way to find out the sequel.

### What Makes Their Outliers Work
- Aggressive counter-positioning ("average 23-year-old," "dropped out," "no rich dad"). The video is as much about shattering the nepo-baby assumption as it is about the liquor store.
- Specific failures are more relatable than specific wins. The drop shipping / Amazon / marketing agency list is exactly the entry-level hustle ladder Marcus (Jay's 24-yo persona) has tried.
- Emotional peaks: "confused and isolated" → "blind ambition to bet on myself." The arc is internal, not just transactional.
