## Creator Profile: @profitplug
**Platform:** TikTok
**Series assignment:** V-Bucks
**Format tags:** E (Educational tutorials), T (Talking-head), D (Sketch/dialogue bits)
**Outliers analyzed:** 10

### Recurring Hook Patterns
- **Pattern 1: "I've been telling you to X for years but never showed you how"** — Example: "I've been telling you to open up a Roth IRA for years, but I don't think I've ever showed you how to do it." and "I'm telling you, invest your money in the stock market for years, but I don't think I've ever shown you how to actually do it." — appears in 2+ step-by-step tutorial videos. Immediately sets the payoff: follow along right now.
- **Pattern 2: Age-framed, fear-anchored hook** — Example: "This is how to invest your first $1,000 in your 20s, so you're not broken in your 30s, or still working in your 60s." and "Three ETFs I'm investing in my 20s. Now, I'll probably hold for life so I don't end up broken my third." and "One of the biggest financial traps that I'm avoiding in my 20s, so I'm not broken my 30s..." — the "broke in your 30s" tag appears repeatedly.
- **Pattern 3: Sketch/roleplay cold open** — Example: "One lottery ticket, please." and "I think I'll take this one. How much is it?" — scene-as-hook (he plays both characters), turns education into entertainment.
- **Pattern 4: Credentials + list promise** — Example: "I'm 24 years old with an almost perfect credit score, but I have over 11 credit cards. Here's to you my favorite cards..."

### Script Structure
- **Typical structure (tutorial):** Hook → "here's a simple step-by-step tutorial" → definition ("First, a Roth IRA is...") → Step 1/Step 2/Step 3 → mistake-warning → mid-script social proof ("if you can sign up for Instagram, you can sign up for this") → CTA to free checklist in bio.
- **Typical structure (sketch):** Character dialogue → ridiculous reveal → breaking the fourth wall with the lesson → follow CTA.
- **Average length:** 40–70 seconds / 250–450 words (longer than most creators on this list).
- **Pacing notes:** He leans hard on numbered steps ("Step number one... Step number two... Step number three"). Each step is a clean subhook. He often ends a step with an aside/joke before the next step to keep energy up.

### Storytelling/Teaching Mechanics
- **Show vs tell:** Tutorials are walkthroughs — likely screen recordings of Vanguard/brokerages. Sketches use costume/location changes. Strong visual variety.
- **Visuals:** Portfolio screenshots ("this is my portfolio at 24"), brokerage signup screens, credit card fans, sunglasses/Versace props in the eyewear sketch.
- **Numbers/receipts:** Very concrete. "$300/month at 8% from 18 = $1.5M vs from 25 = $932K." "77% of my portfolio is ETFs." "$7,000 Roth IRA max." He almost always delivers compound-interest math with both a starting age AND an ending number.

### CTA Style
- **Typical approach:** Free beginner checklist in bio. Exact quotes: "I made a free investing checklist as perfect for beginners. I'll walk you through all the steps. Just click the link in my bio and I'll send you the link. I hope it helps." and "I hope this helps" as a signature sign-off on almost every video. Secondary CTA: "Let me know if you have any questions in the comments down below."

### What Makes Their Outliers Work
- **Tutorials that actually walk you through the exact clicks** — Most "how to open a Roth IRA" videos stay abstract. He names the brokerage (Vanguard), the button ("click open an account, choose Roth IRA"), and the exact ETFs (VOO, QQQM, VXUS). Execution-grade.
- **The "so you're not broke in your 30s" fear frame** — hits the Marcus-persona nerve exactly. It converts a dry topic (ETFs) into a survival warning.
- **Sketch variety as a break from talking head** — Mixing skits (lottery winner, eyewear monopoly) with tutorials keeps the feed from feeling like one-note finance content.
