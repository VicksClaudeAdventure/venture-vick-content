## Creator Profile: @mylenesmind
**Platform:** TikTok
**Series assignment:** Venture
**Format tags:** S (solo reflective talking head) + T (tutorial/brand-integration) — mixed VO-driven outliers
**Outliers analyzed:** 9 (6 usable English, 2 music-only, 1 non-English)

### Recurring Hook Patterns
- **Pattern 1 (direct reflective question to viewer):** "How do you know when you're ready to fall in love again?" / "All right, all right. Be honest. Have you ever been too scared to actually put an effort and try really really hard at something?" / "What makes a movie special?" — appears in 3+ videos. Opens with a question the viewer has literally asked themselves.
- **Pattern 2 (then-and-now identity frame):** "This is me at five years old, sitting at my family's first computer. And this is me at 26 years old, sitting at my laptop like I do every single day." — 1 clean example but structurally distinct. Child-photo to adult-now is a high-retention visual pattern.
- **Pattern 3 (named series episode tag):** "This is episode three of the other side of fear." / "This is episode four of the other side of fear." / "Welcome to episode 7 of the other side of the year." — appears in 3 videos. Named sub-series ("The Other Side of Fear") drives binge behavior.

### Script Structure
- **Typical structure:** Question hook → series tag → "I've been asking myself this lately..." → specific personal context (her mid-20s, her dream job, a movie she watched) → philosophical reframe → open-ended close.
- **Average length:** ~180–230 words (~50–70s).
- **Pacing notes:** Measured, essayistic. She never rushes. Each paragraph lands on a single clean thought.

### Storytelling Mechanics
- **Show vs tell:** Mostly tell, but grounded — she names the exact movie (Pixar's Soul), the exact life stage ("mid-20s"), the exact duration ("one year since the end of my last long-term relationship"). Specificity gives the abstraction weight.
- **Visuals:** Inferred — lifestyle B-roll, laptop shots, walking shots, sunset, movies on screen. Brand-integration videos (Adobe, Firefox, IMAX, codec camera) use on-screen product demos.
- **Emotional arc:** Question → vulnerability → small realization → gentle resolution. She trades catharsis for a settle — "maybe being ready to fall in love again isn't something you feel, but something you choose."

### CTA Style
- **Typical approach:** Almost no hard CTA on reflective content. Ends on a reframe or an unfinished line — "So, until then, I guess I'll keep asking." / "So here's your friendly reminder that it's cool to try hard. So be a try hard." On brand integrations she does use a soft action CTA — "give Photoshop for [brand] a try and take your own reflection boards."

### What Makes Their Outliers Work
- Named essay series ("The Other Side of Fear") converts one-off reflections into a followable thread.
- She asks questions the viewer has literally asked themselves in their own head that week. Mirror effect.
- Brand integrations are indistinguishable in tone from her organic reflective videos — she protects the voice, so sponsored content still performs.
