### Script Structure

- **Typical structure:** Hook (claim/number/confession) → context sentence → itemized breakdown with specific dollar amounts or percentages → "I'll explain why in the caption" deferred-detail hook → mission-statement outro + soft follow CTA.
- **Average length:** ~30-40 seconds (17-30 lines).
- **Pacing notes:** Talking-head, unhurried, but every sentence either drops a number, a tool name, or an insight. Essentially zero filler. He frequently uses "I'll explain in the caption" as a retention-cheat to defer a detail without breaking the flow.

### Storytelling/Teaching Mechanics

- **Specific dollar amounts as proof.** "$200/mo Claude Code, $40/mo Cursor, $900 Perplexity Sonar, $25 Supabase, $12 domain, $100 Apple dev = $1,542 total." Nothing rounded, nothing hidden. The specificity IS the credibility.
- **Before/after metrics.** "Sign-up rate went from 40% to 100%." "$1,800 revenue, $618 costs, 67% profit margin." Always framed as a delta or a ratio.
- **Self-deprecating framing.** "As a solo developer who can't review his own code…" / "I'm honestly embarrassed to admit this…" / "Please learn from my mistakes." He positions himself as the fallible guide, which earns trust.
- **Caption-deferred details.** "I'll explain why in the caption" / "I'll leave more details in the caption" appears in nearly every video. This is a retention + engagement double-dip.
- **Tool name as character.** Claude Code, Cursor, Bugbot, Supabase, Whisperflow, Perplexity Sonar — he gives specific tools specific reasons. Not sponsored. The naming builds trust.

### What Makes Their Outliers Work

1. **Specificity as a moat.** Every competitor in the "build in public" space speaks in vague terms. Chris drops exact dollar amounts, exact percentages, exact tool names, exact bill counts. The specificity is the whole product.
2. **The identity tag works as a brand anchor.** "As a solo app developer with multiple apps…" is his signature opening. Viewers know what they're getting in 3 seconds.
3. **Confession + stakes = retention.** "My app got hacked. Could have bankrupted me." He's not afraid to make himself look stupid, and the financial stakes make it impossible to scroll.
4. **Deferred details to the caption.** This single pacing trick lets him fit 4x more content into 30 seconds without losing the specifics people want.
5. **Every video is a template.** Identity tag → hook → list with numbers → caption defer → mission sign-off. The repetition is a feature — it trains viewers to expect the format.
