### Script Structure

- **Typical structure:** Premise hook (what I built / what it costs) → fast feature-tour OR step-by-step list → one signature line ("I'm Marissa and I…") → soft follow-along CTA. Usually 30-40 seconds and very dense.
- **Average length:** 8-15 spoken lines (~25-40 seconds).
- **Pacing notes:** Every sentence advances either a feature, a number, or a step. No filler, no emotional preamble. The warmth comes from word choice ("girl math means saving up on fonts," "10 out of 10 would go on another fun walk with Claude"), not sentimentality.

### Storytelling/Teaching Mechanics

- **Whimsy + technical substance.** A to-do list that grows flowers. An app you wind up like a music box. A Christmas advent calendar app. The projects themselves are the hook; the tutorial just delivers it.
- **Radical cost transparency.** "$142 on [vercel/railway], $51 on V0, $25 on Supabase, $230 total." Breaks the "I built an app" genre by attaching exact, unglamorous receipts.
- **Signature outro line.** "I'm Marissa and I try to bring transparency to building things on the internet." / "I'm Marissa and make playable tutorials that help you build things with art and technology." — repeats the mission statement as a brand anchor, like a sign-off.
- **Claude / tool name-dropping as character.** "I had to ask Claude for similar alternatives" — tools become collaborators in the story.
- **Show, don't tell, with screen recordings.** Each step is a demo, not an explanation.

### CTA Style

- Light, embedded, follow-along framing:
  - "I build this app in 4 days. Follow along to see my process." (7576731423068015885)
  - "I'm Marissa and I try to bring transparency to building things on the Internet." (7585010509309611294 — functions as a follow-ask by mission)
  - "I'm Marissa and make playable tutorials that help you build things with art and technology." (7600239632890055966)
- The CTA is almost always a mission-statement sign-off, not a request. The ask is implicit.

### What Makes Their Outliers Work

1. **The project itself is the hook.** A to-do list that grows flowers. A voice note you wind up. These are pitched in the first 7 seconds and earn the entire watch by being unique enough to share.
2. **Transparency as differentiation.** Exact dollar amounts, exact user counts, exact tool stacks. In a space full of "I built this in a weekend" vague flex content, specifics are the moat.
3. **Whimsical aesthetic + serious craft.** She ships real working tools but frames them like play. That tonal mismatch is rare in the solo-dev space.
4. **Mission sign-off = brand loop.** Every video reinforces "transparency / playable tutorials." Viewers know what they subscribed to after one video.
