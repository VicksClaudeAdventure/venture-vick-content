## Creator Profile: @bradjcho
**Platform:** TikTok
**Series assignment:** Venture
**Format tags:** S (solo talking head) + D (doc vignettes), serialized daily
**Outliers analyzed:** 10

### Recurring Hook Patterns
- **Pattern 1 (can't-afford + day counter + question):** The rigidly repeated tentpole hook. "I can't afford to propose to my girlfriend, so today's day one of Bordashing for a diamond ring." / "I still can't afford to propose to my girlfriend, so here's day two of door dashing for a diamond ring." / "I can't afford to propose to my girlfriends in today's day 3 of door dashing for a diamond ring." / "I can't afford to propose to my girlfriend, so today's day five of door dashing for a diamond ring." / "I can't afford to propose to my girlfriend for today's day 21 of door dashing for a $6,500 diamond ring." — appears in 7+ videos. Scarcity confession + rising day number + dollar goal = built-in binge loop.
- **Pattern 2 (emotional-rupture opener):** Pure confession stakes, no scaffolding. "I'm gonna get fired." / "I lost the spark in my relationship, and that's the best thing that could have happened." — appears in 2+ videos. Breaks the franchise formula for one-off viral moments inside the same series.
- **Pattern 3 (mini-question pivot):** Inside the hook, he always chains a tiny question that the video will answer. "But why does this girl deserve a $6,500 ring?" / "But why spend 6500 when she'd be happy with less?" / "But what's the biggest tip I've gotten so far?" / "But what's my new year's resolution?" / "But why are working evenings worth it?" — appears in 6+ videos. This is the real structural workhorse.

### Script Structure
- **Typical structure:** Repeating hook → mini-question ("but why does this girl deserve a $6,500 ring?") → specific scene (exact person, exact line) → zoomed-out reframe / philosophical beat → day-count update → CTA.
- **Average length:** ~220–280 words (~60–75s).
- **Pacing notes:** Each video is a single vignette inside a long arc. The philosophical line always lands mid-script, not at the end.

### Storytelling Mechanics
- **Show vs tell:** Scene-first — KFC delivery to elderly couple, girlfriend's handmade Snoopy poker deck, scuffed primark shoes on first date. He reports dialogue nearly verbatim ("I know. I believe that you're going to make it in this world.").
- **Visuals:** DoorDash POV, car interior at night, girlfriend B-roll, receipts, the cats.
- **Emotional arc:** Scarcity → specific tender moment → reframe into a universal truth → recommit. Low-to-high resolution every single video.

### CTA Style
- **Typical approach:** The day-count + dollar-progress + "follow to see how long it takes" — "21 days in, $902.13 at a $6,500. Follow to see how long it takes." / "53 days in, $2,723.9 out of 6,500, I'll let you see how long it takes." The goalpost itself IS the CTA.

### What Makes Their Outliers Work
- The hook is a franchise: same 15 words every video, different day number, different micro-story. Viewers can drop in anywhere.
- He always gives ONE concrete scene per video — never abstract. The universality lands because the specifics are unfakeable.
- Progress bar (days + dollars) creates open-loop tension the algorithm rewards.
