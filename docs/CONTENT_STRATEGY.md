## The Promise

**Jay is a 24-year-old building wealth, a business, and a better version of himself from scratch — and teaching you everything he learns along the way for free.**

The gap between where you are and where you want to be isn't talent — it's information and proof. Jay provides both.

## The Audience

**Marcus, 24.** Scrolling TikTok at 11pm. Works a 9-5, side hustles on weekends. Feels like he's grinding but not getting ahead.

**What Marcus wants:**
- To understand money better than his parents did
- To know how to actually start a business (not just consume content about it)
- To feel like he's making progress toward something
- To stop wasting time and money on things that don't matter
- To find someone who's honest about the struggle, not flexing

**Why Marcus follows Jay:**
- Jay is living the life Marcus wants to start living
- Jay shows HOW, not just WHAT
- Jay's numbers are real and verifiable
- Jay says the things Marcus is afraid to say
- Jay gives him free tools he can actually use

## The Five Chess Pieces

Every video moves one or more of these pieces forward. If a video doesn't advance at least one, it's not strategic content — it's noise.

### Piece 1 — Credibility
Marcus needs to believe Jay knows what he's talking about.

**How we build it:**
- Real numbers in every video (never vague, never rounded up for clout)
- Show the work, not just the result (the AI chat, the spreadsheet, the lease analysis, the code)
- Reference specific frameworks by name (50/30/20 rule, the 3-criteria business validation, the day-counter system)
- Admit mistakes publicly (84% wrong, $34K wasted, hiding for 3 days)

**Credibility is NOT:**
- Flexing results
- Name-dropping tools or people
- Claiming expertise Jay doesn't have

### Piece 2 — Trust
Marcus needs to feel like Jay is a real person who genuinely cares about helping him.

**How we build it:**
- Radical vulnerability (say the thing people are afraid to say)
- Self-deprecating humor (not self-pity — humor)
- Consistency (posting every day, showing up even when the numbers are bad)
- Talking TO Marcus, not AT him (peer energy, never guru energy)

**Trust is the strongest piece right now. Protect it.**

### Piece 3 — Value Exchange
Marcus needs to leave every video with something he didn't have before.

**How we build it:**
- Every script must answer: "What does Marcus take home from this?"
- Value comes in three forms:
  1. **A framework** — a way of thinking about something (50/30/20 rule, 3-criteria validation, the "track every dollar" principle)
  2. **A specific action** — something Marcus can do tonight ("run your numbers through 50/30/20," "open a notes app and write your five ideas," "check if your rent is over 30%")
  3. **A free resource** — a downloadable tool Marcus can use (budget template, spending tracker, business validation checklist)
- Value is embedded in the story, not bolted on. Jay doesn't stop the narrative to teach. He phrases his story so the lesson is built into the narration.

**The reframing principle:** Instead of "It scored them on three things" → "I scored them on the three things that actually matter when you're picking a business idea." Same story. The lesson is embedded, not added.

### Piece 4 — The Funnel
Marcus needs a path from watching to engaging to downloading to following.

**The funnel layers:**

| Layer | What happens | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Watch** | Marcus sees the video | Hook stops the scroll, curiosity gap keeps him |
| **Learn** | Marcus takes away a framework or action | Embedded value in every script |
| **Engage** | Marcus comments, saves, or shares | Comment triggers ("comment BUDGET"), save triggers ("save this"), share triggers (relatable moments) |
| **Download** | Marcus gets a free resource | ManyChat or DM automation delivers the resource |
| **Follow** | Marcus follows for more | Series teases, consistent posting, the promise |

**Free resources (lead magnets) — build these:**

| Resource | Drives from | Comment trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Budget template (50/30/20 spreadsheet) | Anime Budget series, V-Bucks documentary | "Comment BUDGET" |
| Monthly spending tracker | V-Bucks documentary (tracking episodes) | "Comment TRACKER" |
| "Start a business for under $50" checklist | Venture educational moments | "Comment STARTUP" |
| Net worth tracker | V-Bucks investing content | "Comment NETWORTH" |
| "Validate your idea in one weekend" guide | Venture series | "Comment VALIDATE" |

**Start with ONE:** The budget template. Build it. Set up the comment trigger. Test it on the next anime budget video.

### Piece 5 — Identity (The Unifying Thread)
All four pillars must feel like one person living one life.

**How we build it:**

**Consistency of framing:** Every video, regardless of pillar, frames Jay as someone learning and sharing in real time. Default language posture:
- "Here's what I just learned"
- "I'm figuring this out as I go"
- "I'll show you exactly what I did so you don't have to guess"
- "I'm not an expert — I'm just one step ahead and I'm not gatekeeping"

**Throughline details:** Small recurring elements that connect the pillars:
- Recovery counter appearing in V-Bucks videos
- The $37.68 app cost referenced in Authoritative videos about taking action
- Real Estate numbers showing up in investing discussions
- The same spreadsheet tools used across budgeting and business tracking

**Bio:** The promise in one line. Something like: "24. Building my life from scratch. Teaching you everything I learn. Free resources below."

**Pinned posts (3):**
1. Origin story — who is Jay, what happened, why he's doing this
2. Best educational video — what does Jay teach
3. Resource video — what does Jay give for free

## The Embedded Value Principle

**This is the most important section in this document.**

Jay does not stop his story to teach. Jay does not add a "lesson learned" section at the end. Jay does not break the fourth wall to become an instructor.

Instead, Jay phrases his story so the lesson is built into the narration. The viewer learns without realizing they're being taught. This is what makes the content feel like entertainment that happens to be useful, not education pretending to be entertainment.

**The key: it has to sound like Jay naturally talking, not Jay sneaking in a lesson.** If the reframe makes Jay sound like a different person, it's too much. The value should feel like context Jay would naturally include, not extra detail he's adding for the viewer's benefit.

**How it works:**

| Documenting (no value) | Embedded value (natural) |
|---|---|
| "It scored them on three things" | "I scored them on three things — would people pay for it, how crowded the space was, could I build it alone" |
| "I spent two days rewriting the scoring rules" | "I stopped letting the AI decide what was risky and started telling it" |
| "I tracked every dollar last month" | "I tracked every dollar last month and one number I didn't even know existed took up a tenth of my budget" |
| "I fixed the upload flow" | "I used my own app like a normal person and the upload flow made no sense" |
| "The app analyzed a lease in 29 seconds" | "It analyzed a full lease in 29 seconds. That's what a lawyer charges $300 to do in a week" |

The right column tells the exact same story but the viewer absorbs a useful principle without feeling taught. Jay isn't going out of his way — he's just being slightly more specific about what he did and why.

**Rule:** The reframe should never add more than a few words. If you're writing a whole extra sentence to embed the lesson, it's too much. The viewer should never feel the shift.

**Not every sentence needs this.** The hook, the emotional beats, the punchlines — those stay pure story. Only the moments where Jay describes a decision or method get the natural context.

## Maximum Value in Minimum Time

Every second of a video must either move the story forward or give Marcus something useful. If a second does neither, cut it.

**Design principle:** The viewer should feel like they got 2 minutes of value in 30 seconds. Density over length.

**How to achieve this:**
- Specific numbers over vague claims (always)
- Named frameworks over generic advice ("the 50/30/20 rule" not "budget your money")
- One rule per category/section (the anime budget format: every category teaches one principle)
- Cut every word that isn't a number, a character detail, or a lesson
- Let visuals carry information so words can carry story (the dashboard shows the math, Jay's voice tells the story)

## Script Requirement (New Field)

Every script file must include this field before the voiceover:

```
## What Does Marcus Take Home?
Framework: [named framework or principle the viewer learns — or "none, pure story beat"]
Action: [specific thing Marcus can do tonight — or "none"]
Resource: [free downloadable this drives to — or "none, follow CTA"]
```

If all three say "none," the script needs at least one reframed sentence that embeds a takeaway into the narration.

## Measuring Success (Beyond Views)

Views alone don't build the brand Jay wants. Track these:

| Metric | What it tells you | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Skip rate | Is the hook working? | Under 40% |
| Average watch time | Is the body retaining? | Over 50% of video length |
| Follows from video | Is the promise landing? | 0.5%+ of views |
| Comments | Is Marcus engaging? | Growing week over week |
| Saves | Is the content useful enough to revisit? | Growing week over week |
| Lead magnet downloads | Is the funnel working? | Track per resource |
| DMs | Is Marcus reaching out? | Any = good signal |

**The north star metric is follows-per-video.** If Marcus watches and doesn't follow, the promise isn't clear enough or the value isn't strong enough. Fix those before chasing views.

## Roadmap — 100K by January 1, 2027

**Current:** 6K followers (April 14, 2026)
**Target:** 100K followers by January 1, 2027
**Timeline:** 8.5 months

| Phase | Timeline | Follower target | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1** | Apr–May 2026 | 6K → 15K | Nail the promise. Build first lead magnet (budget template). Post 3x/day. Refine hooks using retention data. Find what series pops. |
| **Phase 2** | Jun–Jul 2026 | 15K → 35K | Double down on the series that's performing. Build 2 more lead magnets. Start email/DM list through comment triggers. Establish consistent visual brand. |
| **Phase 3** | Aug–Sep 2026 | 35K → 60K | Launch signature series hard (anime budget or Venture — whichever has the data). Start community (Discord). Collaborate with 2-3 creators in the same niche. |
| **Phase 4** | Oct–Dec 2026 | 60K → 100K | Launch first digital product (budget course or business starter kit). Begin coaching conversations. Build out resource library. Push for viral breakouts during Q4 momentum. |

**The math:** 6K to 100K in 8.5 months = roughly 11K new followers per month average. That's aggressive but achievable if one series breaks out (the anime budget series has the highest TOFU potential) and the lead magnet funnel converts watchers into followers.

**What accelerates this:**
- One viral breakout (100K+ views) that represents the brand clearly
- A lead magnet that gets shared beyond the original viewer
- Consistency — 3x/day posting without gaps
- Collaboration with creators in the 50K-200K range

**What kills this:**
- Posting without the promise (views but no follows)
- Chasing trends that don't connect to the brand
- Inconsistency (gaps in posting destroy algorithm momentum)
- Building products before the audience is ready to buy

**The content never stops being the content.** Jay never becomes a full-time course seller or coach who stops posting. The content IS the brand. The products and coaching grow out of it naturally because Marcus already trusts Jay and has been getting free value for months.
