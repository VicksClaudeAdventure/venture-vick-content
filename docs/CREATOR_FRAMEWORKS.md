## 1. HOOK FRAMEWORKS

### 1.1 🟢 BOTH — Specific, low-word, attention-grabbing hooks
**JUN says:** "Layer ONE hook type" (text/visual/sound). Universal Script Anatomy.
**PBL says:** "Simple, low-word hooks" outperform complex hooks every time. All high-view videos use shorter hooks.
**Action:** Hook under 15 words, lead with the most interesting clause first.

### 1.2 🟢 BOTH — Visual hooks beat plain hooks
**JUN says:** Intentional Visual Framing — choose visuals before shooting; framing affects views.
**PBL says:** "Visual hooks make videos go viral." Multitasking, props, images, multi-split-screen, montage, social proof, final result first.
**Action:** Every script needs a Visual Hook layer in production. The visual is non-negotiable.

### 1.3 🟦 PBL — Four Levels of Customer Awareness (3 videos)
**Framework:** Hooks vary in reach because they target different awareness levels.
1. Product aware (narrowest reach)
2. Solution aware
3. Problem aware
4. Unaware (broadest reach)
**Rule:** Unaware hooks get the most views. Lower awareness = broader reach. Use unaware hooks for unconnected reach (non-followers); product/solution-aware for connected reach (followers).
**Action:** When writing a hook, ask: what awareness level am I targeting? For top-of-funnel, write unaware hooks.

### 1.4 🟦 PBL — Power Phrase Hook Templates
**Plug-and-play opening phrases:**
- "Three levels of [X]"
- "Steal [thing]"
- "Dumb / dumbest / for dummies"
- "From scratch"
- "What's the difference?"
- "[X] versus [Y]"
**Action:** When stuck, plug your topic into one of these phrases and test.

### 1.5 🟦 PBL — Iceberg Hook
**Framework:** Take a tiny ignored detail + reveal massive consequence.
**Examples:** "Why every beer cap has 21 ridges." "Does the amount of water you boil pasta in matter?"
**Action:** Find a small detail in your topic everyone overlooks; reveal its hidden significance.

### 1.6 🟦 PBL — 100 Viral Hook Categories (2 videos)
**~10 categories any viral hook fits into:**
1. Comparison hooks (taste, activity, price, product, time, metric)
2. Tutorial hooks
3. Transformation hooks (personal & client)
4. Educational hooks
5. Common mistakes / myth-busting hooks
6. Tier list hooks
7. Rating hooks
8. Ranking hooks
9. Cost breakdown hooks
10. Challenge hooks
11. Storytelling hooks

### 1.7 🟦 PBL — Comparison Hooks (high performers, 2 videos)
**6 comparison types:** taste, activity, price, product, time, metric
**Examples:** "Being proud of your anger is poor, ignoring it is average, learning is excellent." "McDonald's order comparison."
**Action:** If a topic feels boring, convert it into a comparison hook.

### 1.8 🟦 PBL — Pretty vs Ugly Hooks
**Rule:** Don't focus on aesthetics. Use viral proven hooks even if they look "ugly."
**Examples:** "Everyone tells you to invest in a Roth IRA in 2025 but no one shows you how." "If I woke up 30 lbs overweight tomorrow..."

### 1.9 🟦 PBL — Always Have a Face in Hooks
**Rule:** Human eyes lock to faces faster than any other object. Always include a face in the hook.
**Action:** Every hook frame must include a human face.

### 1.10 🟦 PBL — Same Hook, Different Delivery (3 videos)
**Rule:** Tone, speed, and execution materially affect a hook's performance. Same words can win or lose.
**Examples:** "Sell me this pen" — different deliveries, very different results.
**Action:** Practice multiple deliveries of the same hook before settling.

### 1.11 🟦 PBL — Double Down on Same Hook Repeatedly
**Framework:** When you find a hook that works, repeat it until it stops.
**Variations:** Visual hook / verbal hook / written hook / multiple stacked.
**Examples:** Adam's "If you wrap a pan..." recipe series. Ashton's morning routine series.

### 1.12 🟦 PBL — Hook Quality Tiers (100/100k/1M views)
**Hooks escalate from generic to ultra-targeted:**
- 100v: "Can we talk about one of the hardest things in marriage?" (generic)
- 100k: "How to treat your pregnant wife or girlfriend, vol. 1" (specific)
- 1M: "What do you do if you don't like your wife?" (controversial, ultra-targeted)
**Action:** Push hooks toward emotional specificity and slight controversy.

### 1.13 🟦 PBL — "If You Action, Then Dream Result" Repeating Structure
**Framework:** "If you [action 1] and [action 2], then [dream result]" — repeat with new actions/results.
**Examples:** "If you bite the pen and lift it 100 times daily, your jawline becomes more defined."

### 1.14 🟦 PBL — "Once a [Time Period]" Viral Structure
**Framework:** "Once a day. Once a week. Once a month." Repeating pattern. Currently going viral.

### 1.15 🟦 PBL — CPS (Cuts Per Second) in Hooks (3 videos — high importance)
**Rule:** High-view hooks have ~3-6 cuts (some ~36); low-view hooks have 1 shot.
**Action:** Add a different shot every ~2 seconds in the hook. Medium → close → above → below → long.

### 1.16 🟪 JUN — Human Behavior Writing Rules (Hook applications)
**Rules:**
- Don't say "hey guys / hi everyone" — call out a specific person
- Use "you" for what they want; "I" when highlighting problems
- Make it "us vs the problem," not "you vs them"
**Action:** Address one person directly in the hook, never a group.

## 2. SCRIPT / STORY STRUCTURES

### 2.1 🟢 BOTH — Hook + Value + Story + Lesson + CTA (Universal Anatomy)
**JUN says:** Universal Script Anatomy = Layer ONE hook → Deliver value (clear language, specific avatar, name the problem) → Optional story → Clear lesson/takeaway → CTA.
**PBL says:** Hook + Engaging Format + Script Structure Stack — outlier hook gets past 3s, format gets past value section, script structure carries to the end.
**Action:** Every script must include these 5 elements. Stack them deliberately.

### 2.2 🟢 BOTH — Front-load value, don't bury the payoff
**JUN says:** Retention-First 6-Step Script — don't dump payoff before peak engagement. Re-hook before solution.
**PBL says:** Top % Script Quality Checklist — first line raises curiosity + promises payoff, second line bridges, meat is actionable.
**Action:** First 3 seconds promise payoff. Don't reveal everything in line 1, but signal it's coming.

### 2.3 🟢 BOTH — Templatize and reuse proven structures
**JUN says:** 7x7 Trend-Starter — study 49 videos at structural level, Frankenstein patterns.
**PBL says:** Templatize Viral Outliers (5 videos) — get 5x outlier, double down on hook OR value OR format until it dies.
**Action:** Build a library of proven script structures. Convert each viral video into a fill-in-the-blank template.

### 2.4 🟪 JUN — Retention-First 6-Step Script
1. Hook with curiosity gap
2. Agitate the pain so they lock in
3. Re-hook (question or scene change)
4. Establish context
5. Build to solution
6. End at peak engagement (encourages second watch / loop)

### 2.5 🟪 JUN — 3P Story Framework (Problem, Pursuit, Payoff)
**Framework:** Any 33-second story works with this:
- **Problem** — the struggle/setback
- **Pursuit** — what you did about it
- **Payoff** — the lesson/result
**Use case:** Authoritative scripts, Venture beats, anything personal.

### 2.6 🟪 JUN — Talking Video Script Method (Notebook → ChatGPT → Line by Line)
1. Bullets in physical notebook (free ideation)
2. ChatGPT dictate function (speak ideas aloud, ramble OK)
3. Use a prompt to organize into a script
4. Refine in your voice
5. Memorize and film one line at a time
6. Cut empty spaces in editing
**Rule:** Don't film one continuous take. Line-by-line is more controllable.

### 2.7 🟦 PBL — Top % Script Quality Checklist (Cut Every 2 Seconds Rule)
**Each script element pushes you into a higher percentile:**
- First line raises curiosity + promises payoff → top 50%
- Second line bridges hook to meat → top 30%
- Meat is actionable/measurable value → top 10%
- CTA tells viewer exactly what to do next → top 5%
- Engaging format used → top 1%
- **Bonus rule:** Have something new every 2 seconds (cut, shot, effect, zoom)

### 2.8 🟦 PBL — Bridge Hooks (Mr. Beast Style)
**Framework:** Use mini-hooks throughout the video to bridge sections and maintain curiosity.
**Examples:** Mr. Beast "Whichever one of these three teams moves on..." Bridge hooks connect different parts of a script while creating curiosity for what's next.

### 2.9 🟦 PBL — 5+ Viral Video Structures to Steal
1. **Hand wave structure** — wave hand, show niche recommendations
2. **Three-way split screen** — compare 3 levels of one thing
3. **Redacted reveal** — start with redacted visual, reveal piece by piece
4. **"Wrong girl" trend** — state stereotype + reveal
5. **Bracket trend** — 16 niche items into a bracket
6. **Picture and name** — show + name niche items
7. **"Two people" structure** — "Two people are trying to X..."
8. **"If you X, you will Y"** structure

### 2.10 🟦 PBL — Repeatable Script Templates (2 videos)
**Examples to steal and adapt:**
- "If you drink apple cider vinegar with lemon..." → "If you take creatine..."
- "Two couples are trying to invest..." → "Two business owners post content..."

### 2.11 🟦 PBL — Adapt Outlier Structures Across Niches (4 videos — strong importance)
**Framework:** Find structure → templatize → adjust to your niche.
**Rule:** Works even if outlier is in a totally different niche or language. Avoid fluffy structures with no value.

### 2.12 🟦 PBL — 7 Components Inspiration Rule (3 videos)
**Rule:** Every viral video has 7 components. Take MAX 1-3. Change everything else.
**Components:** Verbal hook / written-visual hook / structure / format / editing / topic / value / CTA
**Action:** Don't copy verbatim. Pick the 1-3 things that made it work and rebuild around your own value.

### 2.13 🟦 PBL — Video Length by Type
- Trending audio + B-roll + text: 3-7 sec
- Q&A / ranking / rating: 15-30 sec
- Light educational with simple structure: 15-30 sec
- Storytelling: 45-60 sec
- Hyper-educational / tutorials: 45-90 sec

### 2.14 🟦 PBL — Niche-Type → Structure Map (3 videos when merged)
- **Visual niches** (see to learn): tutorials, transformations, tips/hacks → voiceover, shot/angle change, visual formats
- **Verbal niches** (hear to learn): tutorials, right vs wrong → clones, talking back-and-forth, green screen, reactions
- **Product niches** (see + hear to learn): comparisons, before/after, storytelling → voiceover, visual, clone, reaction

## 3. STORYTELLING MECHANICS

### 3.1 🟢 BOTH — Show, don't tell
**JUN says:** Story Over Explanation — explain less, share story more = grow.
**PBL says:** Show Don't Tell with Real Evidence — choose a takeaway, collect real-world evidence, present in viral format. Storytelling vs Telling a Story — show what happened, don't just describe it.
**Action:** Every script should SHOW the moment, not narrate it.

### 3.2 🟢 BOTH — Storytelling beats educational lectures
**JUN says:** Storytelling = S-tier in Strategy Tier List.
**PBL says:** "Storytelling videos are MOFU content type that converts to followers." 1+/wk minimum.
**Action:** At least 1-2 of every 7 scripts should be pure storytelling, not teaching.

### 3.3 🟪 JUN — Trust > Authority (Lived Experience)
**Rules:**
- Teach alongside your audience, not at them
- Use plain language (no jargon to sound smart)
- Share successes humbly, not as flexes
- Avoid "you must do X or you're a loser"
**Action:** Avoid LinkedIn energy. Speak like a friend who learned this last week, not a guru.

### 3.4 🟪 JUN — 3P Story Framework (Problem, Pursuit, Payoff)
*(See Section 2.5)*

### 3.5 🟪 JUN — Make viewer feel emotion, not describe it
**From Storytelling vs Telling a Story:**
- Use storytelling structures (NOT generic hook-value-CTA)
- Include past visuals: images, videos, B-roll
- Voiceover with past visuals = great
- Multitasking format > talking direct to camera the whole video

### 3.6 🟦 PBL — Storytelling Format Best Practices
- Pick 1 small story (1 of 7 main short-form story types)
- Show what happened
- Use voiceover with past visuals
- Make viewer FEEL emotions, not just describe them
- Multitasking format outperforms direct-to-camera

### 3.7 🟪 JUN — Bridge-Gap Pivot (A → C → B)
**Framework:** When transitioning content topics, never go A → B directly. Insert bridge gap (C) rooted in your "why."
**Example:** A = scholarship content; B = adulting in your 20s; bridge = "I struggled to finance school myself."
**Action:** When pivoting series, do at least 2-3 bridge videos before the new topic.

## 4. FILMING / PRODUCTION

### 4.1 🟢 BOTH — Engaging formats are mandatory
**JUN says:** Format Combinations — pair expert formats (green screens, demonstrations, split screens, voiceovers) with journey storytelling.
**PBL says:** Format Stacking — normal creators use 0-1 format per video, smart creators use 1+ stacked formats. Talking back-and-forth, multitasking, setting change, clone.
**Action:** Every script needs at least one engaging format beyond talking head. Stack 2 when possible.

### 4.2 🟢 BOTH — Talking head with face wins for trust/conversion
**JUN says:** People Buy Into People — audiences trust profiles built around a real person.
**PBL says:** Talking-Format Beats Audio B-roll for Conversion. Personal Brand Wins Over Faceless in 2026 (2 videos). Always Have a Face in Hooks.
**Action:** Jay must be on camera in most videos. Voiceover-only B-roll videos underconvert.

### 4.3 🟪 JUN — iPhone Camera Settings Checklist
**Settings:**
- Record video: 4K @ 30 FPS (skip 60 FPS unless slow-mo)
- ON: Enhanced Stabilization, Grid, Level, Lens Correction
- OFF: HDR Video (overcomplicates), Mirror Front Camera
- Sound: Stereo for B-roll, Mono for talking/interview/podcast

### 4.4 🟪 JUN — 9 Reusable Clips B-Roll System
**Framework:** Weekly capture session → 3 shot types (wide, close-up, POV) × 3 locations (workspace, wellness space, everyday life) = 9 reusable clips.
**Action:** Once a week, do a 9-clip B-roll session. Build a library.

### 4.5 🟪 JUN — Month of B-Roll in 60 Seconds
**Framework:** Phone on tripod. Cycle expressions across 2 setups:
- Scene 1 (laptop): typing focused, frustrated, looking at phone excited
- Scene 2 (notebook): writing focused, frustrated, surprised
- Save to B-roll library, reuse across formats for the month

### 4.6 🟪 JUN — Intentional Visual Framing
**Rule:** Compare framing options before shooting. The visual choice affects views as much as the words.

### 4.7 🟪 JUN — On-Camera Confidence Tweaks
- Hands above frame, visible
- Drop shoulders away from earlobes
- Camera slightly below eye level (above = makes you small)
- Hold something while speaking (mug, pen) to remove stiffness

### 4.8 🟪 JUN — Pen-in-Mouth Articulation Drill
**Framework:** Before filming → press record → speak about your day with a pen in your mouth for 2 minutes → remove pen and film real video. Forces sharper diction.

### 4.9 🟪 JUN — 10-Minute Random Word Drill
**Framework:** Random word generator → talk for 2 min uninterrupted → watch back, note best idea → edit to 30-60s. Trains mind-to-mouth + produces a postable video.

### 4.10 🟪 JUN — Minimalist Creator Mindset
**Rule:** Equipment isn't the differentiator. Industry trending toward raw/authentic. No fancy gear required.

### 4.11 🟦 PBL — Talking Back-and-Forth Format
**Framework:** Film different videos in different outfits. Slightly angle camera differently for each character. Edit them together. Embrace the cringe.
**Example:** Hair niche creator grew 1M+ followers using this in less-common niche.

### 4.12 🟦 PBL — Format Stacking (Talking Back-and-Forth + 1 of 3)
**Stack on top:** 1) Multitasking (do something unrelated while speaking) 2) Visual props 3) Editing notes overlaid.

### 4.13 🟦 PBL — Natural vs Unnatural Engaging Formats
- **Natural** (hide engagement mechanic): multitasking, voiceover, Q&A
- **Unnatural** (obvious): visual, talking back-and-forth, shot/angle change, clone

### 4.14 🟦 PBL — 9 Camera Angles/Shot Types
- **Low angle** = authority
- **Above angle** = storytelling / appear smaller
- **Top-down** = food, build, tutorial, product
- **Close shot** = secret, personal
- **Behind the shoulder** = computer/phone/mirror view
- **Wide** = setting, edit room
- **Medium** = level with viewer
- **Pan** = environment
- **Extreme close** = one part of something

### 4.15 🟦 PBL — Two Winning Creator Archetypes
- **Winner A (Yapper):** yaps for camera, simple editing, hidden viral hooks, natural engaging formats
- **Winner B (Scripted):** scripted videos, clean editing, obvious viral hooks, intentional engaging formats
**Action:** Pick which archetype Jay is — or mix. Both work.

### 4.16 🟦 PBL — Clone Format Is The Most Viral Format
**Rule:** No matter the niche, account size, or creator — clone format (same person edited twice in frame) is most likely to go viral.

## 5. EDITING

### 5.1 🟢 BOTH — Cuts/visual variety every 2 seconds
**JUN says:** Use shots to interrupt the scroll. Visual variety is mandatory.
**PBL says:** Top % Script Quality Checklist — have a cut/shot/effect every 2 seconds. CPS rule: high-view hooks have 3-36 cuts.
**Action:** Edit so something visually changes at least every 2 seconds.

### 5.2 🟪 JUN — Perfect Loop Editing
**Framework:**
- Step 1: Record the last sentence and the first sentence in one take so they flow naturally
- Step 2: Add a visual loop (e.g., walking out and into frame) by cutting one continuous shot and moving the first half to the end
**Action:** Every script's loop layer becomes an actual edit, not just a callback.

### 5.3 🟪 JUN — Content Style: Colors / Fonts / Visuals
- **Colors:** 2-3 chosen via 60-30-10 rule, golden ratio, complementary, analogous, or triadic
- **Fonts:** primary (hooks/big text), secondary (regular captions), optional accent
- **Visuals:** 2-3 consistent types (screenshots, screen recordings, B-roll, charts)
- **Choose once, use forever**

### 5.4 🟦 PBL — 7 Viral Sound Effect Categories
1. Meme/comedy
2. UI/tech (computer, iPhone)
3. Transition
4. Cinematic
5. Action
6. Cartoon/retro
7. Gaming

### 6.6 🟦 PBL — TOFU/MOFU/BOFU Content Funnel ⭐ (11 videos — highest importance)
**Most-repeated framework in PBL's content. This is the spine of the system.**

| Layer | Goal | Content types |
|---|---|---|
| **TOFU** | Views | Ranking, rating, tier list, Q&A, light educational, fake "what I'd do in XYZ" case studies |
| **MOFU** | Followers | In-depth educational, storytelling about wins/losses, day in the life |
| **BOFU** | Leads | Personal/client before-and-after, fake case studies, in-depth tactical |

**Mix ratios by follower tier:**
- **0-1K:** 3 TOFU/wk, 2 MOFU/wk, 2 BOFU/wk (mostly follow CTAs)
- **1K-10K:** 2 TOFU + 1 double-down, 1 MOFU, 3 BOFU (mix of follow + free freebie CTAs)
- **100K-1M:** 2 TOFU + 1 double-down, 1 MOFU, 3 BOFU (1 follow + 2 paid offer CTAs)

**Content type → funnel layer map:**
- Storytelling = MOFU
- Personal transformations = BOFU
- Tier lists = TOFU
- Day in the life = MOFU
- Light educational = TOFU
- In-depth educational = MOFU/BOFU

**Action for Jay's pipeline:** Tag every script with TOFU/MOFU/BOFU. Aim for the right mix per week based on Jay's current follower tier.
